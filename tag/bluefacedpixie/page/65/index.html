Lucy Fanfare

Lucy Fanfare, originally uploaded by machaggis.

Considering I’m a new father, et al, I won’t elaborate on how many hours I’ve wasted today trying to get a rather geeky cool idea to work in terms of “introducing” Lucy with a little movie title sequence. Rather than, for example, sleeping when the baby is sleeping etc…
The Star Wars TSG program works fine (almost fast on the MacBook Pro) but it left me with 2817 still frame files that I singularly failed to manage (in my change-diaper, do chores, interrupt-driven manner) to do anything constructive with. Multiple attempts at iMovie (on a couple of computers) could not convince it to display frames at 25fps, and multiple attempts to export as a movie from iPhoto or import it as a iMovie slideshow all failed. The best iPhoto effort is a somewhat amusing slow-motion 45-minute sequence – the best iMovie equivalent is almost 8 hours long (glacial!). I did work out how to do it in iMovie, but it involved changing the time for each of the 2817 frames by hand (no bulk update) so I avoided that.
Maybe after this is posted someone will have produced an easy guide for how turn the frames into a movie on a Mac with no disk space, so that I can google it when I have time. Such as when Lucy is three.

Return and Death of freaky-blinky

Freaky-blinky returned to my iPod shuffle this week (as in the one that was replaced last March).

I think it got freaked during the week when, during extensive use as a thumb drive, it started displaying a little strangely on a Windows laptop and wasn’t accessible. When I went to load it up for a run today I got the usual symptoms: wasn’t detected in iTunes, didn’t show as a mounted drive, could be seen as existing on the USB bus in the System Profiler. And, of course, when I went to play it I got the freaky-blinky green/amber combo.

This time however, a quick google found the iPod shuffle Reset Utility 1.0.1 for Mac on the Apple website and a support page that describes the problem. It’s a little unfortunate that the official Apple ipod shuffle troubleshooting pages don’t lead you to this utility though, sending you to a more general iPod troubleshooting page for the same problem description. Confused yet? There is also an iPod shuffle support page, that links to the reset utility, but only as the second topic in “hot topics” so I’m not sure if that’s a permanent link. And it took me ages to find that support page to start with! Thanks goodness for Google; sometimes.

Oh, the iPod shuffle Reset Utility 1.0.1 for Mac? Works like a charm, shame I can’t run for crap this year – but the soundtrack was fine.

Movie: The Promise

We’re currently in the ‘long wait’ phase of pregnancy prior to ‘the arrival’. And we’ve started to watch a lot of movies. I haven’t posted my mini-reviews for a while but thought I would attempt to kick them off once more.

The Promise is an epic (but short) colorful Chinese hero-movie. Which I liked. 4/5 – if you don’t like this genre go with a safer 3/5 but I just can’t bring myself to think the movie isn’t a little better than that.

It’s best watched in Chinese with English subtitles (a fact confirmed when we accidentally started watching it in dubbed English). The story is a little hokey – schoolboy fantasy maps with a superhero-esque race in the mountains, true love, appearances by goddesses, a nice bad-guy. Very satisfying.

Similar to many movies of its ilk the joy is in suspending a lot of disbelief, especially around the fight sequences, and revelling in the flow of the photography and the gorgeous colours and action.

CGI is a little off in the movie, especially in the beginning where there are a lot things attempted that don’t work, followed by a big battle scene that uses the cheap version of whatever software was used in Two Towers and Return of the King and includes the “all falling down” effect from Fellowship of the Rings. There, I think I successfully managed to refer to all of The Lord of the Rings with that sentence.
