Trail Ice Running

Couldn’t chase up anyone to go snowboarding this weekend (despite there being a couple of feet of fresh snow at Snoqualmie and reasonable depth elsewhere) and didn’t want to go solitaire. So I went trail running with Danika out at Cougar.

Couldn’t chase up anyone to go snowboarding this weekend (despite there being a couple of feet of fresh snow at Snoqualmie and reasonable depth elsewhere) and didn’t want to go solitaire. So I went trail running with Danika out at Cougar.

It was great fun – we ran for about 1 hour 45 minutes and there was quiet a bit of ice and shallow snow on the ground. My normal running shoes (Brooks Glycerin 3) did fine and because it was cold it wasn’t that muddy. I’ve gone trail running a few times now, mostly with Danika and it’s my type of sport – compares to road running like mountain biking compares to road biking. (This is to say, not so fast and boring but muddy, engaging and refreshing. I pop my shuffle on when I go road running, I would _never_ do that for trail running.) Some would say this means there is _no_ comparison.

It also reminded me a lot of winter walks and shorter bike rides in Mugdock Park back in Scotland – there was often similar conditions there for the Scottish winter (October-May!).

Today my initial overriding thought was that we should have taken a camera – the sun was up and the light was good and the trees and trail were snowy. And it’s so rare that we get a chance to take photos of each other doing sporting activities.

The best part of the run was the “Deceiver Pass”, a 0.4 mile down and up at about 45 minutes, but it started to get ugly on “De Leo Wall Trail” at about 1 hour and 10. This trail was drier and sheltered but steep and made the heart and lungs work for their money. And just on cue we started to get a little lost. Never fear though – I popped a Gu, we retraced our steps and we spent the next 30 minutes or so running out in the snow. I think Danika even broke sweat.
