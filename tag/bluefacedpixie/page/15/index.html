It may be counting chickens, but if the godaddy forwarding kicks in within the next few hours fortheloveofgoditsmascarpone.com will be the internet’s most recent “Single Serving Site“.

I’m rusty, rusty but in the last 72 minutes to starting to write this post:

  1. Registered fortheloveofgoditsmascarpone.com at godaddy.com
  2. Realized they have less free things than they used to
  3. Forwarded it to http://machaggis.net/fortheloveofgoditsmascarpone.com (still to kick in at this instant) with framing
  4. Registered for AdWords, realized its the wrong thing
  5. Registered for AdSense, have a sneaking suspicion I may have done this before years ago, get told to wait two or three days
  6. Hacked up (with too much google love) the index.html page
  7. Messed around with fonts, styles, underlines and margins
  8. CVS my launch files (all one of them: index.html)
  9. Upload to http://machaggis.net/fortheloveofgoditsmascarpone.com (use this link if the forwarding isn’t working yet)
  10. Comment on Danika’s single serving site Facebook post with cross-advertising for: fortheloveofgoditsmascarpone.com
  11. Write this blog post
  12. Re-edit this blog post with bolds for all the non-linked domain names
  13. Retire to richness, fame, and internet-geekery. I could have been unpacking I guess.

Good night.

Pie vz Cake (where is the Pizza)

Pizza would win, but on the subject of more dessert-like material here is a conversation about Pie vz Cake (*). e.g. a comment:

I’m going to have to disagree with you on the appropriateness of pie on birthdays. I think pie should be a little lower. I have never had a birthday pie.

It doesn’t change the outcome, but it will make your more objective.

I found this when I finally zipped through Facebook checking on what Danika has been saying recently. I commented on a bundle of her things and found her pointing at this funny website (**). I think we also determined we will make a fortune with the website fortheloveofgoditsmascarpone.com.

I was only on facebook because someone had commented on something that was barely news in the house, far less a massive social network, while accepting a friend invitation from someone who was, you know, a living breathing friend.

As a reminder, my 2009 experiment with social media has ended. Facebook lasted 3 months, twitter was more my style but was just another random time suck so I tailed that off at Christmas. It’s always worth evaluating the noise-to-signal-to-distraction ratio of these things occasionally (especially when juggling a baby, do you really need to tweet that?). I still have identities there to stop identity poaching for when I am famous and rich but have no immediate plans to return.

(*) note that in the URL permalink, versus was originally mis-spelled
(**) Facebook. It is nasty and evil and a walled garden. I cannot link to the individual post Danika made here, so trust me she posted about these things.

One Less Car

It’s time to get real about the environment. To do my part, I’ve stopped driving whenever possible. Biking? Not for me, I’m afraid. (Can’t seem to find a comfortable seat.) No, instead I ride around town in my 1960s Aerojet Rocketpack. Sure, my half-mile commute to work now uses enough fuel to drive a Hummer limo across Montana, but it’s all about having one less car on the road.
