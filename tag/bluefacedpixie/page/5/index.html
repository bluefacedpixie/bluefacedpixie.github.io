Throw another kanga snag on the barbie, mate(*) – 16th January 2003

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Andy and Jane’s house, Manly, Australia]

(*) “Place another kangaroo sausage on the BBQ, my bosom buddy.”

Whew! We’ve just returned to Andy and Jane’s abode after two weeks of road tripping in Southwestern Australia. It felt like a compressed version of the beginning of our trip, all those months ago in the USA. We put almost 5000K (3000 miles) on our rental car, drove through areas of scorching heat, camped most nights, and did a fair bit of hiking. We even had to turn back from one destination due to wild fires (which we had also encountered in Colorado).

All things considered, it was an excellent journey. Our first few days were spent driving across the outback for what seemed like an eternity. We spent time in places like Dubbo, Silverton and Broken Hill, all of which have a surprising amount of character. The scenery was gorgeous and reminded us of the Southwest of America. In Dubbo we visited an impressive zoo: although the giraffes and tigers were fun we saw most of the Australian wildlife for real later in the trip (normally jumping in front of the car). Broken Hill and Silverton have hosted tons of movies and commercials – we had a beer at the place where ‘Priscilla, Queen of the Desert’ was filmed. These two towns also had an active arts community and we stopped at several galleries.

After Broken Hill, we drove on to Adelaide as a brief stopover before our eventual destination of Kangaroo Island. Gordon had been before but was not disappointed- this time he got to see the very elusive platypus! The island was really terrific and we saw so many wild animals- kangaroos, wallabies, koalas, seals and, unfortunately, TONS of flies. We ended up staying for two nights and were quite impressed by the relatively unspoiled island. Danika was most unimpressed by the locals though as a bra and two undies went missing from the washing line during our second night camping. We can’t understand – is nothing sacred??

Our plans changed numerous times after we left Kangaroo Island, largely due to bushfires in the Snowy Mountains. Deciding to skip Melbourne, we instead had a very long drive to Canberra, the capital. We visited museums, galleries and parliament buildings but the true standout was the National Museum of Australia – we couldn’t tear ourselves away! We stayed two nights in Canberra, which were slightly surreal with smoke from nearby bushfires obscuring the sky most of the time.

Finally we spent a day and a half driving up the coast to Sydney, visiting various beaches, harbors and sights along the way. The highlight of this journey was a killer BBQ meal Gordon made with steak and shrimp. We also did a pretty cool hike up Pigeon House Mountain near Ulladulla.

We’re loving our time in Australia. An endless stream of amusement is picking up nuances of the language. We’re learning to shorten most English words (i.e. mobile phone is “mobie”, glove compartment is “glovie”, vehicle registration is “rego” and of course, barbeque is “barbie”) and casually use the phrase “No worries” at every opportunity. We’re often seen at the beach or doing something energetic – we’re practically locals! Gordon’s even been mountain biking (the first time since Moab) with his friend Cameron.

On Tuesday, we fly to New Zealand for 5 weeks and this is really the last leg of our trip. In the next few days, we hope to finally SEE Andy and Jane and enjoy the annual Sydney Festival. Hope y’all are having fun in the New Year.

G’day mates,

D and G

11th January 2003 – Kangaroo Island, Australia

Our whirlwind drive of the bottom right corner of Australia continued. After driving west, west, west into the middle-sort-of, we turned left and aimed south for Adelaide. Stopping only to sate ourselves with a swim in the Barossa Wine Valley we then went to Kangaroo Island. I’ve been there before and wanted to take Danika – nowhere is there such a concentrated Australia wildlife experience – koala’s falling off trees, sand dunes, fields of marsupials that bounce – it was awesome. But Middle-Earth was calling and we had to keep moving.

New Year’s Resolutions – 1st January 2003

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Gordon, January 1st 2003, Manly Beach, Sydney, Australia]

We went out on Sydney Harbour on a tall ship for Hogmanay (Scottish: New Years Eve). It was really windy, and although the champagne flowed freely and the food was great the weather was bad enough that the 9 p.m. family fireworks were cancelled – oh no.

About fifteen minutes before midnight however, the winds died down and Sydney brought in the New Year with one of the most impressive fireworks displays we have ever seen. The only possible downside being that no-one had a chance to sing Auld Lang Syne.

When the show was over we headed back to shore. En-route I started the New Year off with a bigger bang than the fireworks. I produced an engagement ring, and in a most romantic manner, asked Danika to marry me. She said yes!

For everyone who hasn’t heard this news yet, we apologise for the electronic format, but it’s impossible to phone everyone from Australia!

We’re really excited about the future but intend to continue our world travels before doing the deed. We will probably marry later in the year when the trip is over, as it’ll be a little easier to organise then. The location is still to be determined, although the petitioning for a wedding in Danika’s hometown (Buffalo, New York state) has already started.

Until then, the adventure continues: we leave Sydney on the 3rd to go into the Outback.

Best Wishes for a Happy New Year,

Gordon (and Danika)

p.s.

On an unrelated technical note, the photos we sent out at Christmas require you to sign up with Yahoo Groups. We didn’t know this and so if you don’t want to do this we’ll work out a way to share some photographs more easily in the future.

26th December 2002 – Manly, Australia

Our first week in Australia captured the essence of the strangeness of this Land Down Under. We had a great time hanging out at the beach on Manly and exploring Sydney and had Christmas in the Sun! It did not feel right, but it felt *good*. We lived a little in luxury, using the apartment/house of Andy and Jane who were, unfortunately for us, Christmassing in England and, fortunately for us, had left their place empty and allowed us to use it! We would meet them later on the trip but the homely luxury of having a base that wasn’t a dodgy Thai lodge or a hostel was great and allowed us to cook and get our life in order. And, as with most of this trip, the Lord of the Rings movies dogged our footsteps and we went to see the opening day of The Two Towers on opening day in Sydney. I had read the book (again) using a large omnibus from Martin (and that Dublin visit oh-so-long-ago) that finally got traded in (at a poor return, that I still feel sore about ten years later) for other books at the 2nd hand bookstore in Manly.

Another day, another continent. – 24th December 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Andy & Jane’s Abode, Manly, Australia]

Merry Christmas everyone!

It seems strange to us, but we’re actually having Christmas in Australia at last! There’s a beach, there’s sun (a few clouds), there’s no rain or snow, and there is a general feeling of kicking back and having a good day. It’s feeling surreal that plans made in Seattle in the spring have finally come to fruition, but we’re loving it.

It’s noon in Australia, and so far we’ve been for a run along the promenade, had a champagne breakfast on the patio, went to the beach for a couple of hours and watched a Christmas Swimming Race. See below for a link to photos of how our Christmas Breakfast looked, and the beach at 9 am this morning: the Australians celebrate Christmas early!! We have plans to make a semi-traditional roast dinner today (ham), laze about in good Ozzie style and to phone home in the evening. For Boxing Day (Americans: the day after Christmas, also a holiday in the UK, Australia etc.) we’re going to a Barbeque lunch with an Australian friend of Gordon’s (met on his mountain bike trip in Peru) and then to see the local opening day showing of “The Two Towers”.

We’ve been in Australia for almost a week now, and we don’t really have much to say! We’re staying in Gordon’s friends apartment near Sydney at Manly Beach. This is a killer abode and is only missing two vital ingredients: the hosts! Andy and Jane are home in the UK for Christmas and New Year so we won’t get to see them until later in January 2003. However, we are conscientious house-sitters and are watering the plants as prescribed. We’re thoroughly enjoying domesticity after almost six months on the road, and have been mostly taking it easy, running, learning to boogie-board, cooking and drinking lots of wine (there is no wine in Asia so we’ve just had two months of beer-drinking).

Our sightseeing has been consumed by several attempts to do Christmas shopping in downtown Sydney; but we’ve managed numerous scenic ferry rides between Manly and Sydney, a walk around Darling Harbour and a short trip to the world famous Bondi Beach (which has no relationship to a certain Danika Bondi Marschner). We have some ideas about doing some more sightseeing in the next week or so before heading out of Sydney and up the coast for a little bit, but we haven’t made any firm plans yet.

Anyway, I think I hear some Christmas carols, and I’m sure it’s time for some sparkly wine, so all the best.

G’day cobbers,

Gordon & Danika

“Smelly t-shirt, just 100 Baht! Toothpaste free!” – 16th December 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Excel Internet Cafe, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia]

Two long weeks since our last newsletter and we’ve done remarkably little in that time! More beach time, a couple of mad days in Bangkok and yesterday we flew to Kuala Lumpur (KL) in Malaysia for an extended stopover before Australia….

After several more beach days at Kata in Phuket, we took a boat to another island called Koh Lanta. Lanta is not as touristy as Phuket. Although we had less to keep us occupied in the evenings we didn’t notice the difference on the beach during our tanning time. While on Lanta we went to the Laanta Lanta local festival one night for some good food, fireworks and traditional performances. This helped us unwind from what had been a terrifying day on a motorbike touring the island. Gordon had demonstrated his developing motorbike skills with extreme driving maneuvers due to pothole-ridden roads, cobra infested pits, dirt tracks, vertical cliffs and an engine with enough power to work an electric toothbrush. All in all a fun day.

The vast majority of our days on Lanta were spent lounging on the beach with a little bit of snorkelling. We also managed to break out of our sedentary traveling mode and started running. So far we’ve been out a handful of times, mostly at sunset on the beach, and we’re feeling much the better for starting to work off some of our weight gain!

After five days on Koh Lanta, we took our last overnight bus to Bangkok. Unfortunately, the bus thieves struck and we lost such bizarre items as a tube of toothpaste, Gordon’s favorite t-shirt and Danika’s new running shorts. Nothing of value was taken but we had to spend a morning tracking down the police station and filing a report. Ugh. Our last day and a half in Thailand were spent in a shopping frenzy as we stocked up on family gifts and cheap cds.

In our first 24 hours in Malaysia we’ve been startled by the contrast between Kuala Lumpur and Bangkok. So far we love it here! It’s clean, modern, well-organized and potentially cheaper than Thailand. Today we went up the tallest buildings in the world (the Petronas Twin Towers), watched the new James Bond movie and had an excellent Indian dinner for $5. Tomorrow we’re even going to have Starbucks!

Our next missive should be from Australia before Christmas, so Best Wishes of the season to you all.

Until then,

Danika & Gordon

11th December 2002 – Ko Lanta, Thailand

Due to a more current, 2012, quick trip to Asia (Korea) I’m a little late in catching up on the log here, but almost immediately felt the warm glow of memory with the log and the sea, sand and surf post due to the wonders of the lady called Judy, who – when we were abandoned at night in Phuket – were saved by a friendly, Thai lady called Judy (who does graphic design with photoshop for German magazines!) who, along with her husband, took pity on us and gave us a ride home – over 20 km. Without doubt, one of the best experiences of our trip.

And 10+ days of log are great – although focusing on an almost tourist beach direction we manage to cover a lot of things – going to Stabucks, a tour of the famous James Bond Island, the Laanta Laanta festival (which the internet tells me is held in March…mmm…the internet also seems to confirm it was on December 8/9 in 2002), more moped and snorkeling (I can’t recollect if Danika had done a lot of snorkeling more this, but it was fun).

And we also crossed the bridge of turning the 6 month trip into something a little more. After Australia we would go to the temperate, Scottish-infused, Lord-Of-The-Rings-was-filmed-here location of New Zealand – pretty much where Cory and Laura are now, almost ten years later.
