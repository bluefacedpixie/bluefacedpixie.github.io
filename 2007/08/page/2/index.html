Stupid Ass Rating Systems

So Cork’d (which I don’t use) has changed it’s stunningly obvious 5-star rating system to a 100-point scale.

Not in itself stupid ass. However when we investigate the “universally accepted 100-point rating system” we discover it actually only goes from 50, increases on this scale 50/60/70/80/85/90/92/96/99 (although you are allowed to use the points in between) and rates the bottom of those point ranges thusly:

* 60-69 Points

Flawed wines to avoid at all costs. Practically undrinkable.

* 50-59 Points

So poor that the 60-69 range is insufficient to describe their inadequacy. Undrinkable.

So basically the 100 points are liberally wasted with everything below 70 being crap, and oodles of oenophiles bitching about subtle one-point distinctions from 90 upwards (and it’s almost a complete stupid ass in itself that the 90 range goes 90-91, 92-95, 96-98, 99-100; what’s with that whole 4 point wedge at 92-95?).

I like my rating system, but am not wed to it. One wine website has a like it/don’t like it voting system – simplicity.

This 100-point system? a) not really a 100 point system more like a 31 point system; b) stupid ass.

Fatherhood 101, 7

I like lists, so here are the 5 things that I’ve fallen back to for the last six months.

In some ways the list could be longer, it could be more elaborate, and there are certainly some standard variations, and it certainly isn’t original.

Whatever, when faced with a crying baby do not panic!, go through the checklist and take appropriate remedial action!

The 5 Reasons Baby is Crying

#1 I’m too cold – put more clothes on, turn up heating, note that crying babies normally heat up like little furnaces, so this should be easy to spot

#2 I’m too hot – see #1, it may be hard to work this one out – but the middle of summer in an unventilated attic bedroom is not the time for flannel and fleece PJ’s – onesies are good enough

#3 I need changed – go on, it’s not that hard, and it’s often entertaining

#4 I’m hungry – where’s Mummy/the bottle etc

My personal favourite:

#5 Unspecified Angst – I still wish this was over. These days it’s more likely to be over-tired or teething or developing separation anxiety, but initially it’s just more about wanting to be back in the womb. There is no solution, I suggest The Happiest Baby on the Block and Baby-Gami and strong Red wine.

The Controversy of Fame

A few weeks ago I boasted of my Fame as Schmap had included a very average photo of mine in one of their guides.

The approach was via flickr, and has p*ssed-off some professional photographers. I watch the blog of one photographer (Darby Sawchuk) who takes great travel shots and he rebuffed Schmap’s overtures and links to a longer article by the Editorial Photographers of the UK about the whole thing.

I love the quote:

Flickr is a mashup of hobbyists who merely want to share snaps of kittens and sunsets and rather a lot of more serious photographers who covertly dream of dumping the day job and becoming pros someday.

The particular comment that makes me think is that if a CC photo is downloaded (and everything is cached by Coral or Google or webarchive, right?) then it remains CC even if you change the copyright. I closed down the copyright on flickr from my generous a-photo-per-day days when I started posting Noodlehead and Lucy, but it makes me wonder what old CC photos will come back and bite me.
