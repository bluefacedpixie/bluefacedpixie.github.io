Where the Buffalo roam… – 20th August 2002

(Being a re-post of ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Danika’s Family Home, Buffalo, NY, USA]

We sent our last update in our final hours in New Orleans. Since then we’ve travelled a good 2000 miles, and not stuck to plan too much! We got some great responses to our Cajun vs. Creole questions. Thanks, guys!

We first travelled east from New Orleans and it was sooo nice on the Gulf that we just had to spend a couple of days on the beach in Florida at Panama City (we stayed at St Andrews State Park in the tent, and it got a little sticky at night). Danika was in her own kind of heaven soaking up the rays on white sand beaches and even managed to engage for a couple hours of kayaking.

After getting a top-up on our tans, we then headed to Savannah and spent the evening there. It was pretty cool, but after New Orleans we’ve started to get pretty maxed out on the whole ‘cute old buildings of the South’ thing.

So we decided to come to Buffalo for Danika’s mom’s birthday and a family reunion at the weekend. But this was a long way from Savannah and we couldn’t miss DC, so we went to the nation’s capitol for a few days. Although some increased security meant that we couldn’t get into the White House, or near the Capitol Building, we pretty much did all the big sights: Washington Monument, Jefferson, Lincoln, and we spent a zillion hours in various Smithsonian museums.

Then we hit the road to Buffalo – a long drive late at night, getting in at 2 a.m.

We’re now leaving Buffalo- after lots of good eats and plenty of time to hang out with Danika’s family. Gordon provided entertainment by teaching everyone Scottish words and saying things like “squirrel” and “cooker”. We’re now heading south back towards Philadelphia and then we’ll loop east to Boston. Our exact route is completely vague, as usual, but we’ll be back in Buffalo for Labor Day. The car stays here and we get ready to venture to Europe, where our trip will completely change- no more camping!

How’s the car doing? We’ve received a few emails on this lately so here are some details. The cute little silver beetle has trekked some 8000 miles on the trip since we left Seattle in July. We’ve had 2 oil changes and today I’ve (that’s G) rotated the tires – and they are definitely starting to look a little worse for wear but have several thousand miles left in them yet. We’ve become experts at packing tents, food, backpacks, sun umbrellas, picnic tables, cooking stoves, folding sofas, etc. into the back of the car on a daily basis. We have photos to prove it :). More than a few bugs have died on our windscreen but the car is as peppy as ever. We’re convinced that if we can survive 8 weeks in the cozy, tightly-packed car, Europe will be a cinch!

So we must be off again. We’re leaving here soon and not even sure where we’ll be tonight. We miss you all and love to hear from you- drop us a line when you can.

D and G
