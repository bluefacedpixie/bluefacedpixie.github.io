6th August 2002 – San Antonio, TX

30 days of unstructured living and USA road tripping.

In 2012 we’ve been on vacation in Scotland and just returned. Hopes to keep this “decade flashback” more current have failed, but the West Coast of America has been thoroughly travelled and now we are in the land of chrome bumpers and steer horns. Three newsletters were posted over this time and will all be re-posted here on bluefacedpixie.

Looking at them, I cannot recollect swimming in Lake Tahoe, and shudder at the bravado of the “wettest night” in Santa Fe (little did we know the camping in NZ would make that a fond, fond memory), and I really wish I had been paying more attention to what bikes I rode in Tahoe and Moab, and countless other details now start to nag at my mind (we watched “Being John Malkovich” in Moab? really? Now “Goldmember”, that I remember…).

Looking back on the log, and even the sparse reminders it contains, I recall how we were hitting our stride as travelers. We had some of our most persistent memories(*) in this early stretch of the trip, and also our first adversity driven decisions (“what do you mean, Mesa Verde is on fire?”).

(*) Saddlebag Lake camping – Trembling Blue Stars on the iPod as the satellites span overhead and we woke to ice; Death Valley is hot; the Las Vegas marriage conversation; a big ass hole called the Grand Canyon; Slickrock; Shakespeare thunder and lightning; g&t’s in a Nalgene (before BPA scares) at the Doubletree pool…

So here is the log brain dump, enjoy the newsletters, and pictures are coming.
