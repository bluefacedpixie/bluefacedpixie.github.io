Aaaahh… sea, sand and surf. At last. – 3rd December 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Let’s Go Travel, Kata Beach, Phuket, Thailand]

We’ve finally ended up back on the beach in Thailand after starting with Koh Samui over four weeks ago. This time we’ve come to the south west of Thailand (the Andaman coast) and the weather has been really nice. So sunny that our plans to go beach hopping have slowed down considerably and we’ve now spent a few days in a nice secluded teak bungalow at Kata on Phuket. Kata is a ‘real’ tourist destination, not a backpacker place, so we’ve been enjoying the civilised comforts of beach chairs, Club Med beach bars, a generally better dress sense and more families than we’ve seen in a while. Unfortunately this is accompanied by some slightly more sharkish practises by the local Thais – taxis, phonecalls and food all cost significantly more than in the rest of Thailand – but we’ve not been caught out too badly now that we’ve been in the country for a few weeks.

Our last email left with us heading to Ayuthaya. We didn’t actually do that ðŸ™‚ Instead we went straight to Bangkok and organised flights to Siem Reap in Cambodia. This is the base town for travelling to see the Ankgors of Cambodia: Angkor Wat, Angkor Thom, Ta Promh…. (the list goes on). We had decided to fly as it was a long, hard and not very exciting alternative to travel to Siem Reap using a combination of bus, boat and truck. And although this would have been an adventure in itself we decided we’d like a little comfort and more time on Thailand’s beaches when we were finished.

Our short visit to Cambodia was excellent: we spent several days staying in a nice guesthouse and explored the temples and ruins by rented tuk-tuk (3 wheeled taxi motorbike) and moped. We then flew straight back to Bangkok and onwards to Phuket where we are now ensconced at Kata.

Phuket is quite civilised and on Sunday we went to Phuket Town and watched Harry Potter 2 at the movies. However because the tourist industry is a little depressed here we misjudged the ease of getting a taxi home after the show was over. However our day was saved by a friendly, Thai lady called Judy (who does graphic design with photoshop for German magazines!) who, along with her husband, took pity on us and gave us a ride home – over 20 km. This was one of the friendliest experiences we’ve had in Thailand and we were most grateful.

We hope all the Americans enjoyed Thanksgiving. Ours was spent at a pizza place in Cambodia where we splurged on a bottle of wine (wine is very expensive in Asia). We are feeling slightly homesick for Seattle this week as the Christmas Bash by superhost RoseMarie (accompanied by the esteemed Bob Hedricks) is on the 7th of December. We will have a fruity cocktail with an umbrella and straw in celebration. Merry early Christmas!

Finally, we have decided to take full advantage of our current employment status (i.e. unemployed wastrels) and extend our adventure. Instead of leaving Australia in January 2003 to return to sub zero temperatures (and many feet of snow) in Buffalo we’ve decided to go to New Zealand for several weeks to see where they made the Lord of the Rings movies. This should be quite good fun and we’re glad we don’t have to think about the end of our trip anytime soon. Although this means that Gordon misses the snowboard season in the US, he probably couldn’t afford to snowboard this year anyway! Plans for return are now early March 2003 – see you then.

Gordon and Danika
