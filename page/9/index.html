Where the Buffalo roam… – 20th August 2002

(Being a re-post of ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Danika’s Family Home, Buffalo, NY, USA]

We sent our last update in our final hours in New Orleans. Since then we’ve travelled a good 2000 miles, and not stuck to plan too much! We got some great responses to our Cajun vs. Creole questions. Thanks, guys!

We first travelled east from New Orleans and it was sooo nice on the Gulf that we just had to spend a couple of days on the beach in Florida at Panama City (we stayed at St Andrews State Park in the tent, and it got a little sticky at night). Danika was in her own kind of heaven soaking up the rays on white sand beaches and even managed to engage for a couple hours of kayaking.

After getting a top-up on our tans, we then headed to Savannah and spent the evening there. It was pretty cool, but after New Orleans we’ve started to get pretty maxed out on the whole ‘cute old buildings of the South’ thing.

So we decided to come to Buffalo for Danika’s mom’s birthday and a family reunion at the weekend. But this was a long way from Savannah and we couldn’t miss DC, so we went to the nation’s capitol for a few days. Although some increased security meant that we couldn’t get into the White House, or near the Capitol Building, we pretty much did all the big sights: Washington Monument, Jefferson, Lincoln, and we spent a zillion hours in various Smithsonian museums.

Then we hit the road to Buffalo – a long drive late at night, getting in at 2 a.m.

We’re now leaving Buffalo- after lots of good eats and plenty of time to hang out with Danika’s family. Gordon provided entertainment by teaching everyone Scottish words and saying things like “squirrel” and “cooker”. We’re now heading south back towards Philadelphia and then we’ll loop east to Boston. Our exact route is completely vague, as usual, but we’ll be back in Buffalo for Labor Day. The car stays here and we get ready to venture to Europe, where our trip will completely change- no more camping!

How’s the car doing? We’ve received a few emails on this lately so here are some details. The cute little silver beetle has trekked some 8000 miles on the trip since we left Seattle in July. We’ve had 2 oil changes and today I’ve (that’s G) rotated the tires – and they are definitely starting to look a little worse for wear but have several thousand miles left in them yet. We’ve become experts at packing tents, food, backpacks, sun umbrellas, picnic tables, cooking stoves, folding sofas, etc. into the back of the car on a daily basis. We have photos to prove it :). More than a few bugs have died on our windscreen but the car is as peppy as ever. We’re convinced that if we can survive 8 weeks in the cozy, tightly-packed car, Europe will be a cinch!

So we must be off again. We’re leaving here soon and not even sure where we’ll be tonight. We miss you all and love to hear from you- drop us a line when you can.

D and G

The Big Easy – 11th August 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Hyatt on Loyola, New Orleans, LA]

Since our last hurried email from the library in Austin, we’ve spent the past 3 steamy days in The Big Easy, traipsing from the Garden District to Uptown to the French Quarter. It’s strangely quiet here in the middle of August but we’ve enjoyed the decreased crowds…

The food has been plentiful- beignets at every turn, muffallettas and oysters, a memorable pistachio cheesecake and of course, Popeye’s. Yesterday, we toured a couple plantations just outside the city- we had a most exceptional tour at one Creole plantation but left with thousands of questions including, “What’s the difference between Cajun and Creole?”

This morning we head out, after a Southern breakfast at The Trolley Stop. Our plan is to drive a scenic byway along the Gulf Coast into the panhandle of Florida. Not sure where we’ll end up this evening but chances are we’ll be back in the tent. Our king-sized bed here at the Hyatt is 3 times bigger than our usual sleeping space. As we move east, we are spending less time camping and more in hotels, and more time in cities rather than the wilds. We’ve probably only got a few nights in the tent left in the US. A big difference from the start of the trip when for the best part of 4 weeks we spent most of our time learning how to car camp and dreaming of hot showers. Not camping also leads to later nights, less cooking, more showers and (oh no) TV in the hotel room!

Our trip so far has crossed two time zones and put over 6,000 miles on the car, visited 10 states and countless National Parks, attended a rodeo in Durango, picnicked at Shakespeare in Santa Fe, cruised the French Quarter in New Orleans, listened to bands in the Continental Club in Austin, biked in Moab, hiked in the Grand Canyon, gambled in Vegas and swam in Lake Tahoe. We’ve immediately converted to being worshippers at the altar of Starbucks (its so much harder to be snobbish about coffee when its the only regular stop for a good double Americano), but apart from that our trip is mostly fast food free. We’ve given in to BK and Wendy’s a few times, tried Popeye’s in the south, but have decided our favourite fast food is a Dairy Queen Blizzard, mmmm, ice cream.

All this talk about food is reminding us we haven’t had that Trolley Stop breakfast yet. So we’re off for some food and will send you our next installment from the East Coast (somewhere). ….

Gordon and Danika

p.s.

One of the hurdles of writing this newsletter for y’all is spelling: favourite and favorite is one of the challenges we’ve had today.

Hot and sweaty in Austin – 5th August 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Public library, Austin, Texas, USA]

Thanks to priceline we’re kicking it back in a deluxe suite in the Doubletree in the centre of Austin. This is the first time we’ve stayed in a hotel since our gruesome Excalibur experience in Vegas, and this time, there is no comparison.

Since Moab, we’ve had a whirlwind tour of the southwest, and started to put some serious road miles in. Our plans to go to Mesa Verde were scratched when they turned us back at the entrance (something about a 3000 acre fire) so we headed north, further into Colorado to Telluride. We’ve subsequently turned cowboy, going to the mining town of Silverton, a rodeo in Durango and ending up in Santa Fe.

We were slightly disappointed with Santa Fe: too touristy and artificially artsy for us. However we did some fun things while we were there – a trip to Los Alamos to see how they made ‘the bomb’, a fiesta in the Jemez Pueblo and Shakespeare at an outside show. The last was exciting: thunder and lightning in the background and then finally a holy torrential downpour: the show was cancelled and we went back to our tent for the wettest night of our trip so far.

The ‘big drive’ from Santa Fe through Texas was pretty good, we stopped at the German town of Fredericksburg in Texas Hill Country – not what we expected at all.

Now we’re in Austin, which is really cool. We’ve just finished a tour of the state capitol building and decided that we want to become Texans.

Bye for now,

Gordon and Danika.

Mountain Bike Mecca – 27th July 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[grand county library, moab, USA, 27th July]

Yosemite, Death Valley, Vegas, Zion, Grand Canyon, Arches, Moab

We’ve certainly made good use of our National Parks Pass by doing a grand tour of the west since our last message from Reno. Unfortunately Las Vegas is not a National Park yet, but the slots took their toll! We arrived in the Mountain Biking capital of the US (Moab, Utah) last night and are planning to hang here for a few days. Hopefully meeting up with Gordon’s friend Brad. Our breakneck pace of the last week or two has been fun but it will be nice to stay in the same place for more than 2 nights.

The heat has subsided somewhat from the temps we experienced earlier in the trip. Driving through Death Valley, it actually hit 50 degrees Celsius/122 Fahrenheit! Two nights ago, we slept through a thunder and lightning storm in Monument Valley. It’s amazing how used to these awe-inspiring sights we’ve become. Even the Grand Canyon failed to inpsire on the first glance but made a ‘grander’ impression after hiking down and camping the night. We also found one of the prettiest waterfalls of the trip so far (Yosemite, pah) in the Grand Canyon beside our campsite: Ribbon Falls. Who would have thought?

Not only has internet been scarce the past few days, but we’ve had no cellphone coverage either! Technology withdrawal hurts!

The next few days will bring mountain biking galore (for Gordon) and a whole lot of relaxing (for Danika). We then continue east through Colorado and south to New Mexico. Our spirits are high and we’ve been eating like kings. We look forward to seeing more friends and relatives in the coming weeks and promise to try and shower before we arrive.

In the mean time…

g and d

6th August 2002 – San Antonio, TX

30 days of unstructured living and USA road tripping.

In 2012 we’ve been on vacation in Scotland and just returned. Hopes to keep this “decade flashback” more current have failed, but the West Coast of America has been thoroughly travelled and now we are in the land of chrome bumpers and steer horns. Three newsletters were posted over this time and will all be re-posted here on bluefacedpixie.

Looking at them, I cannot recollect swimming in Lake Tahoe, and shudder at the bravado of the “wettest night” in Santa Fe (little did we know the camping in NZ would make that a fond, fond memory), and I really wish I had been paying more attention to what bikes I rode in Tahoe and Moab, and countless other details now start to nag at my mind (we watched “Being John Malkovich” in Moab? really? Now “Goldmember”, that I remember…).

Looking back on the log, and even the sparse reminders it contains, I recall how we were hitting our stride as travelers. We had some of our most persistent memories(*) in this early stretch of the trip, and also our first adversity driven decisions (“what do you mean, Mesa Verde is on fire?”).

(*) Saddlebag Lake camping – Trembling Blue Stars on the iPod as the satellites span overhead and we woke to ice; Death Valley is hot; the Las Vegas marriage conversation; a big ass hole called the Grand Canyon; Slickrock; Shakespeare thunder and lightning; g&t’s in a Nalgene (before BPA scares) at the Doubletree pool…

So here is the log brain dump, enjoy the newsletters, and pictures are coming.

Greetings from Reno – 17th July 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Washoe County Public Library – Reno, NV]

Who ever thought we’d end up in Reno? So far our plans have changed several times and we’ve significantly altered our route from the original. We’ve added San Fran and Sonoma and extended our stay in Tahoe. Currently, we’re enjoying the generous hospitality of Michael Moore, who is a new resident of Reno. It’s hot and dry here but there’s no sales tax and Lake Tahoe is a stone’s throw away. It’s not half bad…

Today is Day 10 since we’ve embarked on this adventure. Surprisingly, things have not gone exactly as we expected but we’re having a great time! Experiencing a bit of Internet withdrawal, sadly enough, but have discovered we actually LIKE camping! Gordon has been cooking up some fantastic camping meals, including an amazing salmon dinner in Tahoe a few days back. The Beetle is holding up well despite a minor incident with the passenger seat on Day 2, where it turned into an ejector seat (not unlike the James Bond Aston Martin).

We’ve put over 1600 miles in already and have done quite a bit in 10 days! Our favorites include the Columbia River Gorge and Highway 35 along the East side of the Cascades. Bend, Oregon was perhaps the friendliest place we’ve been (with all of California ranking low on that scale). Tahoe has also been incredible and we’ve done everything from hike to mountain bike to swim in the lake. Although it’s cold, there’s something remarkable about swimming in Lake Tahoe.

Our next week’s plans include a couple days in Yosemite, a night or two in Vegas and on to the North Side of the Grand Canyon. It’s amazing how free our lives are just now. Neither of us are missing the routine we had in Seattle although I think we’re both a little bit homesick.

Cellphone coverage has been patchy, but in the US travels our number is (206)….

We’ve also just discovered that mac.com is going to start charging for service, so we’ll both be changing our personal email accounts (again) over the next few weeks: but the cutoff is September 30 so we may not do this until we leave the US. Stay tuned for details…

Until then we are: machaggis@m… and danikam@m…

The score so far: 1600 miles, 3 fastfood stops, 5 starbucks coffees.

’til next time,

Gordon and Danika

16th July 2002 – Marriott, Reno, NV

There was a day, when still fit from running a marathon in Hawaii at the end of June, Danika and I did blast our way up a 9000 ft mountain (from a pretty high Lake Tahoe start) and down again without thinking much of it. We then ate awesome mango salmon cooked on the hibachi (mini gas grill), with a neighboring tent that was running a generator to run a pump to make sure their air mattress was hard.

The next day I rented a bike (can’t really remember what, might have been front suspension only, maybe a Specialized, wish I had taken notes) and did some mountain biking on the Flume Trail above the Lake.

Then we drove down into Nevada and went to crash with our friend Michael who was working with Microsoft in the desert.
