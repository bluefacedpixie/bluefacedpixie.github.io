29th October 2002 – Rome, Italy

Today we went to Pompeii, which was excellent, and we also had some wrestling with booking transit to Paris and ended up with a somewhat unplanned new plan to fly. Rome is pretty intense, and a little like London has a lot of “density” to it – not needing much area to cover a LOT of history and/or sights. We stayed in a hotel from Rick Steves’ which is where the train drivers on night duty sometimes stay, thankfully we were not asked to time-share our room during the day. It also had a TV – a rarity on our trip – and I distinctly remember watching Moby on it on MTV.

Did someone say pizza… – 28th October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Navigator 50 Cyber Cafe, Rome, Italy]

Buongiorno! It’s been too long since we wrote our last newsletter. We did try yesterday but the computer ate it, so we’re trying again today. Apart from that we have no real excuse – internet isn’t quite as easy/cheap in rural Italy, but mostly it was just because we’ve been too busy eating pizza, gelato and drinking cheap red wine.

Since Nice we’ve spent almost two weeks in Italy. We’ve done a whistlestop tour of many places, celebrated Danika’s 26th birthday in at least three different towns and arrived in Rome yesterday where we will settle for a few days.

So what have we seen and done??? Our Italian experience began with a mad 24 hours in Venice. We looked at the canals, took a gondola ride at night, ate Italian food, stayed in a nice hotel. It was really good, and the gondola ride was not as cheesy as we feared it might be, so we can recommend it to anyone as a once in a lifetime experience.

We continued on to Florence where we were based for a few days. We got Renaissance overload here, but thoroughly enjoyed it (the sights included the Duomo, David, Uffizi gallery, the Ponte Vecchio), and also went to Pisa on a day trip. The leaning tower was really cool, but Pisa was really *only* about the tower, so we didn’t do much else there (except eat pizza).

From Florence we toured Tuscany as best we could by train and bus and ended up going through Umbria. We went to Siena, San Gimignano, Assisi, Orvieto, Civita. Although we missed a lot of the less mainstream sights (and a lot of vineyards) by not having a car we managed to cram a fair amount in – next time a car is a must!

Out of all these towns we have a few selected memories for you:

Siena is the cutest town of our trip, Santa Fe loses big time. We stayed in a hotel run by Nuns with a view of the Duomo. We had bad colds but succeeded in eating well. By ordering things in Italian, we accidentally ended up with things such as liver, horse and lower bovine intestine but did escape the tripe! We also discovered one of our favorite Italian restaurants, serving dirt cheap food and wine as well as having really entertaining clientele.

San Gimignano has the most tourists, but we did get a great bottle of Chianti for about 4 bucks. Gordon had his favourite gelato here.

Civita is really hard to get to by bus. It took us almost all day to get there and back. But it was worth it. It’s a little pedestrian town reachable only by footbridge, and occupied by only 15 old Italian women. It isn’t even in the Lonely Planet guidebooks! Going to Civita was meant to be a birthday treat for Danika, but after all the fun with the buses, I think the better treat was the bottle of champagne in the hotel once we got back!

Now we are in Rome. Yesterday we toured the ruins of the ancient world (colosseum and roman forum) and although mostly rubble they were really interesting, big and fun. Gordon’s really glad he wasn’t a gladiator though.

Over the next few days we’ll do the rest of Rome, go to Pompeii on the Eurostar and then head to Paris. Then its Thailand and onwards.

Hope everyone is doing well and enjoyed their summer. Keep on writing as we’re getting further away as the days go by.

Ciao, G & D.

25th October 2002 – Orvieto, Italy

Getting to the end of our time in Italy we start focusing on small, somewhat famous medieval towns. We had the famous towers of San Gimignano, the pilgrimmage of Assisi and ancient Etruscan city of Orvieto. We also had an important lesson about being carless in Europe – when you rely on a funicular to take you to the town on top of the hill (from the town on the bottom of the hill) make sure you know when the last ride home is. I like the abbreviated note and will resolve how we were stranded on the next update.

22nd October 2002 – Siena, Italy

….Bed Bugs! The shock, the horror. We survived. Funnily enough there was a National Strike that day, which meant some things were closed. On day 104 we went to the Uffizi which was one of the more awesome galleries/museums of the trip, to say nothing of just walking around in one of the most photogenic towns of our trip yet (Florence!).

After a day trip to see a leaning tower we headed to Siena, starting to play chicken with the late-Autumn European weather but I am totally blanking on it, so will need to go find some old pictures.

17th October 2002 – Florence, Italy

This is an installment – 3 countries, recovered wallets, wild crazy vomiting, the 100 day mark, 2 train overnights and Italy, Italy, Italy.

We stayed in a lovely hotel in Barcelona (Hotel Jardine) and took the train from Spain to France during which the afore-mentioned vomiting occurred (and yet we still ended our stay in Nice with eating some of the most iffy food of the whole trip (cheap bouillabaisse)). Nice was an experience with a dingy Rick Steve’s hotel (Hotel Petite Louvre), which was not a homely place to be sick but Danika brought in Chinese soup, and a spectacular show of a tighty-whitey (Danika had to explain the term) sunbather on the pebbled beach. But then we moved on to Venice.

Venice was great, although we had some food challenges and looking back I find it somewhat hard to believe we were only there one night but then we went to Florence (I’m assuming by train). And it was photogenic. And on the first night we stayed on a hotel with a mural on the ceiling and….[to be continued]

It’s nice in Nice – 15th October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Internet Cybercafe, Nice, France]

We are just at the end of three relaxing days in Nice. We arrived early Sunday morning on an overnight train from Barcelona. Gordon was quite ill when we arrived and spent the first day mostly in bed. (He’s feeling better now, thanks to Danika’s attentive care :)). Nice has been lovely and sunny- it’s even been warm enough to lay on the beach in bathing suits! We’ve seen a few more art museums and walked the length of the shore endlessly. Unfortunately, we can’t afford to stay here; Nice is far more expensive than Spain. The other downside of this cute seaside town is the extraordinary amount of dog poo on the ground! We’ve adjusted to walking with our eyes on the ground at all times to avoid stepping in it – Gordon has been unlucky…

Since our last news, we spent three days in Barcelona, which Danika deemed her favorite Spanish city (G is partial to Toledo). Despite some difficulties with lodging, we had good food, more cheap wine and saw lots of cool Gaudi architecture. The city seems to have quite a buzz to it and we enjoyed walking up and down the main street – Las Ramblas. It even had a Starbucks as well as a good Picasso museum. En-route to Barcelona we spent a day in Granada, though it was slightly disastrous – got soaking wet at the Alhambra, spent hours trying to find an Internet cafe, lost Danika’s wallet (later recovered), and so on.

In any case, we’re off to another new country tonight. Yet another overnight train – this one to Venice, Italy (perhaps the only place that’s even more expensive than Nice!). We’ll be spending roughly two weeks in Italy with our typically open schedule – the main objectives include Florence, Tuscany and Rome.

Danika sends her thanks to everyone for their wishes this past week. It’s nice to hear from you directly even though we don’t always have time to write back to you individually.

Oh yes, and for all those in suspense, Gordon did get a haircut. Not in Sevilla, but in Barcelona. He still doesn’t know the Spanish for it, but ‘mucho’ is for when you want more off, and ‘grande blanco’ has something to do with the increasing amount of grey hair he has.

Au revoir,

D and G

8th October 2002 – Granada, Spain

A busy Spanish week saw us moving from Madrid through Toledo and Sevilla to Granada. On the way Danika’s grandfather passed away, which tested our travelling resolution and we went to Flamenco for his distant funeral (we last saw him at the family reunion in Buffalo – well worth that East Coast dash). Spain is, by this time, just awesome as we get into the flow of travel (and the Spanish trains were so far great) but already the whistlestop nature of our trip through Europe is beginning to show as the clock starts to tick on Spain.

Holy Toledo Batman!!! – 7th October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Dodgy Internet Cafe, Sevilla, Spain]

We’re now in our third Spanish city. We’ve been to Madrid, Toledo and currently we’re in Sevilla. Our plan is to stay here for a couple of nights and then head out. Probably to Granada and then Barcelona.

Since our arrival in Spain we’ve started to passively smoke about 30 cigarettes a day because all Spaniards smoke like industrial chimneys. This is much worse than Ireland where the Irish only smoke like little chimneys.

In Madrid we went to the Prado and the Sofia museums to see most of the major Spanish artwork and also hung around a fair bit. We had tapas almost every night, and started to get into the Spanish way of staying up, and getting up, late.

We took the train to Toledo (the ancient capital of Spain) and it was great. It was full of twisty little streets and Moorish architecture and castles and lots of very bloody Christian history. We also got out best food so far in Spain – although the tapas have been very good we’ve been struggling to get good big meals we like.

While we were in Toledo we also got some bad news from home as Danika’s grandfather passed away. He had been ill for some time, but it was still a shock when it actually happened. After trying to see how feasible it was for Danika to go home for a bit we decided to continue travelling, and came to Sevilla on Sunday. Danika has called home a few times and our thoughts are with the whole family.

Today it’s Monday, so everything touristy is closed. This is giving us some time to do chores, laundry etc. Depending on how brave he’s feeling Gordon may even attempt to get a haircut.

Adios Amigos,

Gordon & Danika
