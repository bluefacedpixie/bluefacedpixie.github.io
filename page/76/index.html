Business in a twisted world

In case you were distracted by “real world” politics this week, you may have missed the deal where Microsoft pays Universal (who sells 1/4 the world’s music or so) a cold hard $1 on each Zune they will probably fail to sell well. Ostentatiously this is a dream come true for record labels who would like a revenue slice on every radio, CD player and 8-track player you own because, you know it plays their music. They argue, for iPod, Zune etc, this covers the cost of the pirated music you will definitely install. Which leads to the very natural question: So does this Zune royalty cover all my pirated music, or just the tracks you presume I’ve stolen from Universal?

There are so many geek threads on this (mostly MS-hating, Apple-loving, but there’s more to it than that) that the one above will have to do as a summary.
