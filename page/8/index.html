3rd October 2002 – Madrid, Spain

And we finally reach mainland Europe. From Mum’s birthday in Scotland to a great time in Ireland with Martin to the “Hostal Martin” in Madrid we had a fun time in the not-quite-proper Europe. Not elaborated on much in the notes is the trip to the Aran Islands, which involved buses, trains, puddle-jumping airplanes and mini-vans, but was a great, if very long day of tourism (and had us hanging about in Galway for a bit too). Accomodation in Madrid was a little on the horrid side and the shock of surviving in Spanish, and being in a big city was a wake-up call; but as we progress through Europe, things improve.

Dear Mom, Guinness is good for you! – 1st October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Martin’s House, Dublin, Ireland]

The Emerald Isle, Land of Guinness and Leprachauns, The Four-Leafed Clover, etc…..

Well our time in Ireland is nearly over, and we decided to send this newsletter before we head off to Madrid this afternoon. This way we manage to send a letter from each country we’ve been too!

Since our last email (from the library Gordon used to live in as a kid) we’ve completed our grand tour of Scotland. For the last few days we visited some of Gordon’s friends and relatives, and in doing that also managed to go to the west coast of Scotland in Ayrshire and to the home of golf in St. Andrews. Fully stocked on fish suppers, good Indian and haggis neeps and tatties (to say nothing of oatcakes, Irn Bru, venison, dodgy Scottish beer, cider, caramel shortbread, Cadbury chocolate, Kit Kats, etc) we flew from Edinburgh to Dublin.

We’ve had a grand time in Ireland. Staying with Martin and Jane we’ve been to the Guinness Brewery, seen Trinity college, the Irish National Museum and Gallery. We also took a long day trip across Ireland to Galway and flew (6 minutes each way) to the Aran Islands on the west coast. These islands were impressive in their rocky desolate nature – although it’s no wonder these are the people who invented Aran sweaters. Apart from donkey and boat, we used almost all forms of travel for this day trip: car, train, coach, plane and public bus. Impressive, eh?

For our time in Scotland and Ireland we’ve had nice dry weather. For people not from these parts, and who did not suffer the dismal endless rain of ‘summer’, let me assure you that this was very welcome and we feel that the weather gods have smiled on us for this part of the trip. We’re definitely looking forward to donning summer gear again, as the daily temps in Madrid are around 29 C (84 F) this time of year.

Now we head off to Spain for our next experience: strange people who don’t understand English/Scottish/American no matter how slowly and loudly you talk.

We’d like to leave you with one of our favourite Irish quotes (from the Irish Literary Pub Tour, and the distinguished Brendan Behan (sic)):

“The Irish Man is the only Man in the world who would crawl over twenty naked women to reach a pint.”

Adios amigos,

Gordon & Danika

I would walk 500 miles… – 23rd September 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Kilsyth Public Library, Scotland]

..or at least drive 1000 more.

We have been blessed with excellent weather for our time in Scotland. We’ve managed to see many of the big attractions and put another 1,000 miles of driving in – this time in Gordon’s mum’s wee car. Our first couple days were spent in Kilsyth at Gordon’s childhood home. We were treated to lots of yummy soup and caramel shortcake – Margaret’s specialties. Spent a couple of days in Glasgow – the highlights being a fabulous meal at G’s favourite restuarant and a night of traditional Scottish dancing (ceilidh dancing). Unfortunately, Gordon’s kilt is in Seattle, but it was an exciting evening anyway.

For the past week we’ve been on a grand tour of Scotland. We started with Ben Lomond and then spent the night near Fort William. We continued on to the Isle of Skye, which is infamous for bad weather. It didn’t rain, which means it was quite good, and we had fun hiking in the Quirang. We also had ‘a wee dram’ at the Talisker whisky distillery. Oh yeah, the scenery was quite good too. After two nights in Skye, we hit the quaint town of Ullapool, where we saw the excellent Scottish Ensemble perform. Then a breathtaking drive through Northen Scotland and a stay in one of the northernmost towns (Durness) before heading south to Inverness. Two nights in Edinburgh, including a most terrifying ghost tour, and we’re back in Kilsyth!

Danika’s having a great time here and frequently proclaims her newly adopted country – Scotland! We’ve been practising accents and she’s mastered such local tongue twisters as “It’s a braw bricht moonlicht nicht, the nicht, it’s a richt ya ken?” and “There’s a moose loose aboot the hoose”. Two more days in Scotland and we’re off to Dublin to see Martin and Jane, and the Guinness brewery.

Until the next time,

Slainte Mhath,

Danika and Gordon

17th September 2002 – Ullapool, Scotland

We pick up our tale with a touch of class with the BT Scottish Ensemble in the West Coast of Scotland. We finished our USA leg in NYC, overnighted to Britain, spent time with Diana and friends in London and then moved north. We had had insane hot Chinese food in NYC with Megan (who just got married in Bellingham!), had the essential Scottish curry and ate fish and chips on the Royal Mile. Oh and did Belgo in London. And danced a ceilidh in the now closed Riverside Club. And ate what was to be my last meal in Mitchell’s Charing Cross (also closed). Definitely, in more ways than one, this trip was a bit of a swan song of Scotland for me – I never knew it at the time and at least Danika caught a glimpse of some of the highlights.

Other experiences included the classically wet trip to Skye (I promise, one day I will go there when it’s nice) including what I think ended up averaging out (once price-per-person and $/Â£ were taken into account) as one of, if not the most expensive accomodation of the whole trip. Cute though.

England, New England…. – 29th August 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Hyatt Hotel, Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA]

We were all set to leave Buffalo last Tuesday with the car packed and still no idea where we were going…at the very last minute, we decided to take 2 days to drive to Philly with a stop in Allegheny State Park. We camped in an area that was completely deserted and left wondering if perhaps the campsite was closed. In any case, it was a most peaceful night and we headed off through the back roads to Fallingwater. Fallingwater is one of Frank Lloyd Wright’s masterpieces built on top of a waterfall and was a great tour in a beautiful setting.

Passed through Amish country (Danika had to be dragged there) and arrived in Philly with many complaints about Pennsylvania driving, quality of roads, speed limits, etc. We spent three nights in Philadelphia (Philly) – eating as much as possible (cheese fries from Ishkabibbles, Philly Steak Sandwiches, Philly Breakfast Sandwiches) and visiting Danika’s friends.

Want to know what a Philly Cheese Steak Sandwich is? A long soft white bun with thinly sliced fried steak, browned onions and cheese (normally cheap squeeze cheese) on top to make a big, tasty, gloopy mess. Excellent. However when visiting more of Danika’s family friends in New Jersey we had it beat. A New Jersey Philly Cheese Steak from Gaitano’s which has all of the above plus potatoes and peppers. Did we mention Gordon’s Gap jeans have shrunk at the waist?

After Philly we headed to Cape Cod. This ended up being a mammoth adventure – we had to drive through Staten Island at night due to a diversion, slog through New Jersey and New York City traffic in the dark and finally slept in one of the few campsites in Connecticut. At least we got to see the new skyline of Manhattan lit up in the night. Red, white and blue Empire State Building is very cool.

In Cape Code we hung out on the beach and Gordon met up with Todd, an American friend he had met in Australia many years ago. We also had our first full blown ‘lobster in a restaurant’ experience in Provincetown: lobster bibs and lobster-cracking tools, bits of shell flying everywhere! Provincetown was where the Mayflower landed in America (although it settled in Plymouth) so our tour of the historic sites of the US is mostly complete now.

To finish up our New England experience we’ve come to Boston, but we’re getting a little jaded now and it just feels like another city – although it definitely feels like an old Eastern city. Gordon, for once, can’t point at every building and comment how ‘young’ it is. Many parts of Boston were built in the 1600s making most of the area as old as Glasgow and Edinburgh (and the rest of Europe). We did go to Harvard though and it’s pretty cool, and watched ‘My Big Fat Greek Wedding’. Good family viewing for everyone.

We’re going back to Buffalo now (for the holiday weekend and Gordon’s birthday), and were planning to go via the Adirondacks for some last camping. But it’s raining pretty heavily just now (just like England and Seattle) so maybe we’ll have to modify our plans or just tough it out.

Hope everyone’s well, please email us as we’ll have some consistent internet time in Buffalo before we head to New York and then Europe.

Until the next time….

Gordon & Danika
