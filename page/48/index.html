TV: Doctor Who Christmas Special 2007 – Voyage of the Damned

TV: Voyage Of The Damned (Doctor Who Christmas Special 2007) – wikipedia

2/5 – once you give up your fond childhood memories of Doctor Who being scary and serious time-travel science-fiction and succumb to the new nuevo-Brit-rah-rah-rah Doctor Who of Russell T. Davies it’s pretty easy to enjoy the show.

This episode has Kylie Minogue (too celebrity for Doctor Who, ever), superbad robot effects and a futuristic Titanic, and the Queen (in passing). It’s nonsense of course, but an enjoyable – once was enough, and I could maybe watch it again – entertainment nonetheless. The bfp rating system is extremely harsh sounding in such circumstances. But really. A 2 is all you get. Torchwood is a 1, remember.

Movie: Live Free or Die Hard

DVD: Live Free or Die Hard aka Die Hard 4

Now this is a holiday movie. Ok, so it was the July 4th 2007 US blockbuster-to-be, but it’s still fun. The spirit of the original Die Hard, without the gross deathcount of the successors. A little touch of heart from Bruce. Not a 4 primarily due to the issues around multiple locations and pretty implausible tech aspects. But anything with a star jumping onto a jet plane and sliding off after avoiding being blown up by air-to-surface missiles has a certain suspension-of-disbelief.

And to think I rented this at the same time as Surviving Christmas.

Live Free or Die Hard: 3/5

Movie: The Family Stone

DVD: The Family Stone

So, it turns out the was a Christmas movie after all! Although it was the Noel corner of the video store it doesn’t mention Christmas anywhere on the cover or in the pictures – and as the Noel section also offered Pieces of April, it could have referred to any difficult family holiday situation.

But, no, this was a standard twisted relationship drama set around a liberal household in New England for an unspecified Christmas a few years past. I won’t really spoil it for you, except to say that D almost ended up liking Luke Wilson, and the younger sister was both not pleasant and quite pretty (according to imdb she’s Maxim’s #68 in 100 sexiest movie stars even though she’s only been in the movies for a few minutes).

It is a romantic comedy though, and I was slightly bored most of the way through, although I did laugh out loud (LOL!) when the naked turkey hit the floor, and the strada scene was fun and the Saab scene.

So if you’re stuck, go for it. You could do worse.

2/5, life is short.

Movie: Surviving Christmas

DVD: Surviving Christmas (imdb).

Danika’s instructions were to rent something mindless and Christmassy. We agreed we couldn’t rent Love Actually again – as we rented it last year and there is always a danger of ruining your favourite Christmas movie. I think we were both hoping for something classic, or good, but no.

Rain City Video had a nice little Noel section, filled with lots of kids movies and empty spaces. They also had VHS, and I was tempted to go for the old tape of Scrooged (which D cannot remember seeing) but instead went for The Family Stone (unwatched so far) and Surviving Christmas. Ben Affleck, The Sopranos, the blonde from Third Rock from the Sun Married With Children (or whatever) who is Rachel’s sister in Friends – a sure fire hit!

Um, noo. 4.8/10 on imdb.com and I think that’s generous. I sure wish I’d been totting an iPhone and thought to look the score up in the store.

Anyway, we blazed through it (apparently the studio cancelled the black comedy script and the cast ad-libbed through the movie), electing not to pause when we went to the bathroom. And we’re done.

1/5 on the grand scale (aka “Run for your life”) – I don’t recommend it, and I don’t think it’ll be a Christmas tradition.

Also mentioned:

  • Love Actually
  • Scrooged
  • The Family Stone

Update: Apparently Christina Applegate was in Married With Children, so I need to watch more 90’s TV.

Christmas Quarantine

Christmas Eve: “Well I guess we’d better buy a turkey, or pizza.”

It’s now official. The pede has applied bilateral sanctions on little L and her battle with The-Virus-That-Attacked-Christmas.

Our increasing list of missed engagements can now be projected into the future rather than being last minute cancellations. So evening drink soiree, Christmas brunch and Christmas dinner now join Saturday brunch, skiing and Christmas lights on the casualty list.

Technically D & I can still circulate within the social world, but we’re alternatively sleep-deprived, worried, crotchedy and occasionally vomit-covered (sometimes all at once too): we’ll probably keep such excursions limited to the minimum. Such as trips to the store for chocolate, alcohol and drugs filling prescriptions.

Christmas traditions start in the strangest ways, and in some sense I’m pretty resilient to the ongoing challenges – it is our first Christmas Eve all in our little house in front of the tree after all, so who knows what annual activities this will be the genesis of.

Merry Christmas everyone!

The W’s.
