Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

As Danika already blabbed, I rode the bus to work for the whole week so that I could read Half-Blood Prince. It was close call yesterday when our friend Kristen got on the same bus, and then I had Date Night after work, but I managed to finish it off before midnight.

I’m not a rabid fan, but my gut feeling is this is the best yet. Although Order Of The Phoenix was probably a slighly better story, the Half-Blood Prince was better edited and (for adults) more funny (although the fun is mostly about teenage relationships rather than the kid jokes). Very little can be said about the book without spoilers, so I’ll summarize: tightly written/edited, not overly long for no reason (the print is big), fairly consistent and fun story (although drops the DA element of last book strangely), good character development, not a _naff_ ending. Definitely worth bussing it for a week (especially given the fortunate turn of crappy weather Seattle gifted us with 🙂 ).

If it takes two years to have #7 with the same level of quality, then let’s wait (well George RR Martin just made us procrastinate forever for A Feast for Crows and that’s a book that ended up split in 2!).

For my next trick I am going to stick with fiction, as I’m reading too rarely to do a work book or non-fiction just now. Probably Pompeii by Robert Harris.

The score on the door for Half-Blood Prince? 4 out of 5.

I hate iTunes/Airport Express part 1

I’ve resisted this for too long, but many months have passed and I am still unhappy.

I have an airport express that refuses to play music without sometimes skipping (dropouts are the approved internet search term). Somedays it is fine, others it is bad. It’s normally bad late at night and when I have company.

It skips when I stream via ethernet from a Mac Mini to a WRTG54 router to the express, and also when I stream from a Powerbook locally to the WRTG54/Airport express (well with WDS you never know which one you are talking too) and also from the Mac Mini via ethernet/WRTG54/wireless and then to the WRTG54/Airport Express.

I have tried many internet searches and fixes (wireless B, new channels, different encryption…). No joy. So I am proceeding with two tactics a) blog it – who knows what search will give me the perfect solution in my comments (hint, hint) b) I’ll try and borrow another airport express and try that. Who knows – it may be hardware after all.

This is a love/hate/love/love/hate relationship.
