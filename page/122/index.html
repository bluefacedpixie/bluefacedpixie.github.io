19th Mar 06: Summer is coming

19th Mar 06: Summer is coming, originally uploaded by machaggis.

For various reasons I ended up close to the house today. It was a perfect spring day – blossoms, blue sky, frigid cold in the morning and out of the sun. I did yard work before lunch and then managed to convince myself that the “fix bikes” part of my to-do could be completed on the back patio. Regular maintenance for my commuter, a little bit of love for D’s rarely-used bike (in preparation for the Tour De Lopez – hopefully) and then I started to resurrect my mountain bike for the coming summer.
I think I may have the brakes working, I’ve cleaned off the congealed mud from 5/6 months in the basement and it’s pretty much ready to go. Although it’s starting to look like it needs another round of replacement parts (wheels, cables, brakes etc) I’m trying to not justify buying a new bike.

Slick Tires and New Brake Pads

Last Friday I got soaked and even sleeted on (in Seattle!) on my ride home from work.

So this week I tried to ride smart. No joy though. Yesterday I took the bus as it looked pretty wet. 4 blocks from my house it was dry and by the evening it was almost balmy and a classic Seattle spring evening.

Today I biked with the threat of rain and was engulfed for most of the ride to work. Ok, this is an exaggeration(*), it was only wet. But it was an interesting ride (and back home this evening when it was wet on the ground but thankfully not falling from the sky) as my bike was all over the place. I had switched from my knobbly ‘cross tires to slick road tires for the Chilly Hilly but hadn’t noticed much difference to my commute apart from a little burst of speed. My slicks are still pretty fat – 32cc – so I’m blaming it all on the new brake pads I installed in the early a.m.. I guess they make a difference after all.

On reflection, this is a pretty meaningless blog – oh well!

(*) exaggeration: strange looking word – I spell-checked it twice.

Environmental Twinge

I was going to take the car to drop Woken Furies off at the library when I finished it tonight. It’s already two days overdue.

Book: Woken Furies

I’m just coming out of a massive sci-fi book kick that I haven’t been sharing. I picked up a bundle of books to take to Mexico a month ago, some long-awaiting sci-fi series and by chance (as choice was dictated by the second hand book store) some other easy-to-reads that all ended up as sci-fi. I immediately returned from vacation only to find that the Woken Furies (Richard Morgan) hardcover had arrived in the library. More sci-fi.

I’ve consumed this over the last week or so – and it’s really good, bloody noir sci-fi with Takeshi Kovacs, ex-Envoy, gun for hire, ex-criminal, confused psycho, once more the anti-hero. The only issue here is that he becomes more anti-hero and less sympathetic to the reader in every book. In Altered Carbon he was the flawed action figure and the sci-fi, near-future universe of the Protectorate was being established. In Broken Angels, Kovacs was more than a little psychotic, but we were off-planet, the space opera was pretty encompassing and there were Martians. It’s really hard to tell which of those books were the best – they were both 4’s in their own way.

Woken Furies isn’t quite so good – the setting is established and Morgan pushes it as far as it can go. Kovacs is basically now mental and it’s hard to care for him, and the book reads like an endless series of set-pieces with a few particularly gory fight scenes. Not that it isn’t good – if you only read one near-future sci-fi this year this would not be a bad choice. But the warning signs were there – the blurb on the backcover seems more enamoured with the fact that Altered Carbon has been optioned by Joel Silver than the story within or the author.

But, 3/5 in the grand scheme of things.
