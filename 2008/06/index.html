Over the Bridge

My fourth Seafair half-marathon!

This year there was a new course – more interesting, but harder. We ran one-way from UW to Bellevue – going over the 520 bridge, which was great fun. There were more hills and less dullness than the old Bellevue-centric route, but we also had a hot day – starting about 70° at 7.15 am!

My time(*) was down from the last few year (1 hour, 46 minutes, 18 seconds) including a (now regular) unscheduled pit stop – but with the new route and the hot, hot, heat I’m happy.

Danika did the full marathon in 4 hours 13 minutes – fabulous! I kept her company for the first 200 yards and the last 1/2 mile.

Results so far:

  • 10-Jul-05 Seafair Half Marathon 1:48:02
  • 9-Jul-06 Seafair Half Marathon 1:42:40
  • 8-Jul-07 Seafair Half Marathon 1:42:38
  • 29-Jun-08 Seafair Half Marathon 1:46:18

Danika tells me I was 180th out of over 1000 men doing the half-marathon and 36th out of 186 in my age group ðŸ™‚

(*) Interestingly enough the official results page this year posts finish times based on clock time and not on the little chip we pay for and run with – but give the 2 mins 25 second delay in crossing the start line. So I took that off. That’s the whole point of chip times – it stops people from fighting to cross the start line first – we leave that to the Kenyans and fast folk (like Edward Kiptum who ran the marathon in 2 hours 20 minutes 28 seconds).

More on the Speccy

I got all misty eyed reading the ZX Spectrum wikipedia article I just linked to – and here is a BBC article recapping on it all!

For context – I had a little black-screen-and-green-cursor experience with a (I believe) Research Machines RM 80Z at school, an exposure to a ZX81 at the BB’s and then the glory that was the Spectrum. School progressed without me to BBC Micros, all my friends wend Vic-20 or Commodore-64 (considered upstart American machines… apart from David Old who went for an insanely great little computer called an Oric Atmos (*) that was never popular) and years later I was programming for Amazon.com. I still remember writing games on the Spectrum while Eastenders was playing on TV.

What was this post about again? Oh yes – the Spectrum!

I loved Chuckie Egg.

(*) Serious brain drain just went into remembering the name and make of that computer.

Indy 4

Indiana Jones 4 (movie): 3/5

I saw this the week it came up, and I went in dreading what could happen to the movies/series (but it couldn’t be worse than Indy 2, right?). I enjoyed the movie which was an unintentional balance of goodness, badness and fun.

Many people do not see it this way, and there is a hilarious spoof on the script – abridged.

The link is below, but before you go there here are 3 things:

  1. You need to be ok with the F-word
  2. It tells the story of the movie so only read it if you’ve seen the movie, or..
  3. read it if you don’t want to see the movie because you think it’ll be bad

Here is an excerpt:

HARRISON FORD

City of Gold? IsnÂ’t that what Nicolas Cage was looking for in National Treasure 2? Are we seriously getting the plot for our shitty sequel to Indiana Jones by ripping off the plot of a shitty sequel to a shitty knock-off of Indiana Jones? I feel like that should unravel the space-time continuum or something.
