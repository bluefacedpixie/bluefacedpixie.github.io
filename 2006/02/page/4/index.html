Food: Maximilien’s in the Market

I normally leave it to Danika to do the food posts but tonight’s food warrants a mention.

A classic over-hyped French restaurant in the tourist-friendly Pike Place Market. That’s all I ever thought Maximilien’s was, even though I’d had lunch and snacks there. I even went with Mum while doing the Market last September.

We went tonight to have some food, managed to sneak into the upper “view” bar after sunset but before the happy hour (5-7) finished and had frites, 2 doses of mussels, French onion soup, bread and a couple of beers and wines. All of which were great. And the bill was less than $30. This is not an over-hyped tourist restaurant – even though it is a tourist-trap.

I think we have to go back.

Maximilien’s in the Market: 4/5

Movie: Transporter 2

Dialogue that makes you want to weep.

Stunts that are so unbelievable that the suspension of disbelief has to be suspended.

The phrase “bollocks” used many times by the audience.

However, we just watched the DVD because I was allowed to rent an action movie. The storyline was hokey and any of the finesse of The Transporter was lost in relocation to Miami, Florida. However there were some of the coolest stunts* I have ever seen in this movie – and the really unbelievable ones were cool too, but mostly silly** – although I don’t watch that many action movies these days (apparently XXX is madness too). The movie varies between a 1 for corny and a 4 for coolness. I can’t really say anymore about the movie because there is really only a basic two-themed story (Man on Fire bleeds into something more) and one subplot (apparently the Mom is hot) and even describing it gives too much away; but really it’s all about Jason doing his own stuff while wearing a Blues Brothers suit.

Transporter 2: 3/5

(*) jumping between two cars hitting each other head on and then landing on the crashed bonnets/hoods

(**) really too many to count, but how about a fist fight on a flat-spinning Lear jet?

Briefly and gloriously online once more

I’ve just returned from a business trip and am astounded by how good it feels to be eating breakfast at home and surfing on my computer. Oh well, just a geek at heart I guess (or just liking my comforts).

Last night the flight into Seattle from a hard cold snowy 5 a.m. (east coast time) start in Canada was gorgeous. The sky was clear and Mt. Rainier was catching glimpses of red sun, Seattle was laid out like a satellite map – Ballard was there, and Discovery looked small. One day I may even try and take a photo of it all. And it was so nice to re-unite with D.

I’m soon to be offline for several days again, so I will plan of posting my little stock of photos of the day before I do, and hopefully I’ll get a chance to to fulfil my Durango story promise.

The hell of treadmills

I ran for 30 minutes on the tread this morning, which is the first time for a long time.

I learned to re-appreciate the merits of treadmills: the pacing is more constant than anything you would get out in the real world, being able to grab your heart-rate is positively scary. Just for the record, apparently max is 220-age which means I was 4 above this morning on 5.5 incline at 7 miles per hour.

However I also re-affirmed my hatred of this type of torture. It was boring, hot, hard on the knees and there was nothing except bad Canadian TV. to watch.

But there is 10 inches of iced snow outside so it was good to be moving.

Here’s my tally for last week:

Mon (30th): night run the Fiore loop in reverse (5 miles/42 mins)
Tue: bike to/from work
Wed: nada
Thu: bike to/from work
Fri: bike to/from work
Sat: Snowboarding at Stevens
Sun: nada (lots of flying)
