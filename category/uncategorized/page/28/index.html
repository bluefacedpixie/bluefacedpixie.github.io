The tip of the iceberg

I spent way too long on Sunday doing non-exciting computer things with machaggis.net. I should have been doing a little web project for a friend which is long overdue but instead I had to tackle issues surrounding running out of hosted webspace for machaggis.net. I’m hosted on luxsci.com and only pay for 100Mb of space, which for the first time has started to get tight (mostly due to transient email).

So I spent 3+ hours syncing CVS directories, deleting stuff and generally being paranoid about completely erasing and restoring machaggis.net. If you see anything strange or broken let me know.

As all of this is completely invisible I decided to finish up the fruitless Sunday by tweaking bluefacedpixie. I’ve been getting some comments that planetdanika is ‘nicer’ and ‘cleaner’ than bfp. Some of this is, I’m sure, unjustified and probably just relates to to the use of san-serif fonts, bigger text and cool pics (which I took I must add). But I have succumbed, and admit that bfp was a little bit of a playground for design for a while, so I’ve switched most fonts to sans except for the main stories (serif is meant to be easier to read in the long run, even if it doesn’t seem so at the time, and I like Georgia!). I’ve also pulled the optional formatting for blog this, upped the font size and gutted the sidebar. I also hacked the wordpress calendar so that the current date would appear in bold.

So the tip of the iceberg from today’s boring geekery and hackery is some lipstick on the blog. Enjoy. (Comments welcome).

“A Wounded Mastodon” (Book: Big Weather)

Martin thinks the Robert Jordan series

rolls on like a wounded mastodon down a gentle slope

I think he hasn’t read Big Weather, which in one book wants to make you suffocate yourself by swallowing a whole honeydew melon and gives a whole new meaning to speed reading – every page has the same word on it somewhere (“tornado”). It would have (and I think it did) made a good 3000-word article for Outside magazine – endlessly repeating with no panache for 300 pages was just no fun – weather is not that exciting. Unless you’re talking about the recent Seattle rain. No, I was wrong, that’s boring too.

Big Weather – 1/5.

Damn Fine Hogmanay

I’ve not had much luck with my four previous US Hogmanay’s…

I’ve not had much luck with my four previous US Hogmanay’s – for two of them I/we’ve been loaded with the cold, and for the first I tried to First Foot some friends only to discover they didn’t know the tradition! Last year Danika and I tried a getaway to Vancouver, but as they don’t have fireworks it was the first year we hit the hay more or less before the Bells.

Of course, the most successful Hogmanay ever was our engagement in Sydney, so there is a lot to live up to!

After the party we intended to attend to welcome in 2006 was cancelled Danika and I had few fallback plans. We liked the idea of going to the Tractor Tavern in Ballard to see The Long Winters bring in the New Year, but the show was sold out. So we decided to play bah-humbug with a bottle of champagne at home and proceeded to have a fun 31st of December.

First we went snowboarding, and then went for Indian at the India Bistro. This was great – we had been outside, energetic, were tired and completely stuffed – perhaps a new tradition in the making. Then we swung down by the Tractor with our friends Morgan and Kristen and managed to pick up tickets to The Long Winters after all! So we ended up being up for the Bells, although we were pretty tired and bailed after the band played the majority of it’s main set. As we didn’t intend to be out we have no photos, but they would probably be too blurry to recognize anyone anyway.
