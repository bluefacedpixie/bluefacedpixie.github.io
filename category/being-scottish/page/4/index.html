The next time I emigrate to America

I sure am going to get rid of those UK shares.

This arrived from Switzerland today.

The non-assenting shareholders of Egg plc in or resident in the United States of America, Canada, Australia, Japan or any other jurisdiction where extension or acceptance of the Offer would violate the laws of the jurisdiction.

I knew I was in trouble, or alternatively, I was going to lose money. And then I read this as the third paragraph:

Notwithstanding the references to the Offer in the enclosed statutory notice, the Offer is not being made, and will not be made, directly or indirectly, in or into, or by use of the mails, or by any means or instrumentality (including, without limitation by means of telephone, fax, telex or other forms of electronic communication) of interstate or foreign commerce of, or by any facility of a national securities exchange of, the United States of America, Canada, Australia, Japan or any other jurisdiction where extension or acceptance of the Offer would violate the law of that jurisdiction, and is not capable of acceptance by any such use, means, instrumentally or facility. Doing so may render any purported Offer invalid.

Eh?

I love capitalism, shareholding and the fact our pensions/”future golf careers” rely on this stuff.

I think it translates to:

If I could tell you it would be illegal. You would lose money. I’m not going to tell you. It will lose money. If you ever even mention (or blog) that I may have mentioned this (via a form of electronic communication or telex (really?)) then you will owe the third child’s third index finger as compense, we will accept body parts of near relatives and friends. And we will charge you for it.

Oh, and if you live in countries we are friendly with (US, Australia, Japan et al), then these rules apply. If you’re hiding out in some loser country we don’t like, then sell your shares and make some money.

Governments.

Wrong Weekend

Ooops.

While I was whining (sp?) I would miss the next 6 Nations rugby match while at Whistler this weekend (“Shame” I hear you cry – not!), it turns out I was a weekend out and Scotland did the business last Saturday while I was in bed/at Besalu/doing yardwork without my attendance.

Premodernist brought this to my attention.

However, there is a small miracle happening here. Since coming to the US, Scotland (in its many guises) has been crap at sport. Especially the only one I occasionally watch which is rugby. Now it appears we are getting better. A good win on the first game, and the third game being a surprise against the arch nemesis. And to prove I’m not totally anti-English, they’re definitely one of the four (if not two) best teams in the world just now and we’re not. But we may be lucky, and getting better.

We won.

Burns Night

Out over the Forth

Out over the Forth, I look to the North;
But what is the north and its Highlands to me?
The south nor the east gie ease to my breast,
The far foreign land, or the wide rolling sea.

But I look to the west when I gae to rest,
That happy my dreams and my slumbers may be;
For far in the west lives he I loe best,
The man that is dear to my babie and me.

Damn Fine Hogmanay

I’ve not had much luck with my four previous US Hogmanay’s…

I’ve not had much luck with my four previous US Hogmanay’s – for two of them I/we’ve been loaded with the cold, and for the first I tried to First Foot some friends only to discover they didn’t know the tradition! Last year Danika and I tried a getaway to Vancouver, but as they don’t have fireworks it was the first year we hit the hay more or less before the Bells.

Of course, the most successful Hogmanay ever was our engagement in Sydney, so there is a lot to live up to!

After the party we intended to attend to welcome in 2006 was cancelled Danika and I had few fallback plans. We liked the idea of going to the Tractor Tavern in Ballard to see The Long Winters bring in the New Year, but the show was sold out. So we decided to play bah-humbug with a bottle of champagne at home and proceeded to have a fun 31st of December.

First we went snowboarding, and then went for Indian at the India Bistro. This was great – we had been outside, energetic, were tired and completely stuffed – perhaps a new tradition in the making. Then we swung down by the Tractor with our friends Morgan and Kristen and managed to pick up tickets to The Long Winters after all! So we ended up being up for the Bells, although we were pretty tired and bailed after the band played the majority of it’s main set. As we didn’t intend to be out we have no photos, but they would probably be too blurry to recognize anyone anyway.
