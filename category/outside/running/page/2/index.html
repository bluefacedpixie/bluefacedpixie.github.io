The hell of treadmills

I ran for 30 minutes on the tread this morning, which is the first time for a long time.

I learned to re-appreciate the merits of treadmills: the pacing is more constant than anything you would get out in the real world, being able to grab your heart-rate is positively scary. Just for the record, apparently max is 220-age which means I was 4 above this morning on 5.5 incline at 7 miles per hour.

However I also re-affirmed my hatred of this type of torture. It was boring, hot, hard on the knees and there was nothing except bad Canadian TV. to watch.

But there is 10 inches of iced snow outside so it was good to be moving.

Running Muscles

Until today there has been 27 straight days of rain in Seattle. If we get more than 33 then we beat the record set in 1950. However today seemed to be dry – there was the light grey shine of dry concrete and some blue sky peaking out from the clouds.

Until today there has been 27 straight days of rain in Seattle. If we get more than 33 then we beat the record set in 1950. However today seemed to be dry – there was the light grey shine of dry concrete and some blue sky peaking out from the clouds.

I took the opportunity to run from the house in the afternoon around the “Fiore loop”. This is a c. 5 mile run from our house that heads to the bay, along the marina, up an evil hill and set of steps to the Cafe Fiore and then wends it way back to the house. My goal one day is to run this in less than 40 minutes. Last year I got very close, and Danika may even have accomplished this, but as she turned into a running rocket I’m not surprised. Today was not that day for me.

It’s amazing how after a few weeks of slow time at holidays followed by changing to another sport (snowboarding) muscles can just decide they don’t remember how to work. Even though I still feel relatively fit, and haven’t been doing too badly biking to work, and not hurting too much from boarding, my legs felt like lead today, my heart was going to burst and my lungs were really, really small. But it was good to be out in daylight, in the dry, and there were many fellow residents walking dogs, running and generally standing in the way. I don’t feel too bad at all (now that I’ve stopped).

Sun: run fiore loop, 44 mins

First Run of the Year

Motivation wasn’t lacking today. Although I welched on heading out to the mountains to snowboard by myself, I headed off to Discovery Park after Danika went to work despite the fact the rain was coming down consistently.

Motivation wasn’t lacking today. Although I welched on heading out to the mountains to snowboard by myself, I headed off to Discovery Park after Danika went to work despite the fact the rain was coming down consistently.

It was warm but wet, although in the trees the rain wasn’t so heavy. I ran the loop clockwise from the north carpark once, with a big diversion down to South Beach/lighthouse and then the North Beach. The tide was in so I ran on the trails rather than the beach itself. I stopped to take a couple of bad pictures for a photo per day on the way to the lighthouse, and as soon as I put my camera away an eagle drifted by about 30 feet away. That was kind of special.

I was out for 53 minutes not running very fast, but I don’t know the official distance (i.e. how far Danika tells me it is) as I added in the beaches.

Seattle is crazy though – I wasn’t even alone, there were plenty of other runners out!

First Impressions: Brooks Cascadia shoes

My new running shoes are Brooks Cascadia – my first ever trail running shoes. The shoes are ugly enough to make you puke. They are so ugly that apparently REI made Brooks produce a grey version just for them. But at least you don’t have to worry about the red/yellow look clashing with any particular outfit – they clash with _all_ your outfits (the girl’s version is a pretty blue though).

I had to choose between the Brooks and and Montrail Masai‘s. The Brooks are trail running shoes made by a road running company, the Masai’s are trail running shoes made by a hiking boot company. The Masai’s were grey (a bonus) and had a good mid-foot gusset. But they were hard under the toe, cut high in the back and I’ve been running in various Brooks since I came to the US (apart from a failed Asics experiment) so I went with the Brooks.

Took them for a spin around Cougar doing (what Danika and I are now calling) _Scott’s Loop_ for about 100 minutes yesterday. Stuck my ever reliable Superfeet inside them as insoles. And I liked them. Like all new shoes that fit well they feel supportive and _fast_.

To be honest the ground was harder than concrete (36°F) and I didn’t notice anything particularly _trail runnerish_ about the shoes. On one occasion I kicked a rock and liked the rubber toe, and they maybe gripped a little better on an uneven corner or two, but I think it’ll take more mud to determine how much better they are than my road shoes.

Did I say they were _fast_?
