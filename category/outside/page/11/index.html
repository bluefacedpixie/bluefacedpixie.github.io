Ride: Zipping Tiger

Another blast around Tiger tonight – this time with Adrian.

Legs were a little tired after Fiore runs this weekend but managed to climb the fire-road in 29 1/2 minutes, and overall we zipped about for 1:18 rolling.

Best conditions and riding overall since the first time out this year: dry weather with a little wet on the trails, some good mud and nice non-dusty dry trails. A few scares (there seem to be more drops every week) but everything in control.

Ride: Tiger Mountain

What a difference a week makes.

Last night I went to Tiger with Tony (friend of Nick’s) and it was fun to be out with a new rider. Tony hauled his Ti-Cycle around the course and was in great shape for someone who finished Ski to Sea‘s mountain bike leg last Sunday.

My new derailleur cable worked pretty well and I got up the fireroad in under 30 minutes! All in the road, Preston and NWTT only took 1 hour 15 minutes of rolling time so I was happy.

The weather was overcast and 75°F so it was a little humid. It started to rain as we rolled into the parking lot at the end of the ride. There was a BBTC recruiting tent out that night and I met an old friend, which was cool. Although I feel guilty about not joining the org.

And for those wondering, last night I tried the Rudy’s with graduated orange – it worked pretty well although it was still a little dark as the evening went on and my head started to droop (as the dark at the top is *dark*).

Four Tigers down this year.

Ride: Tiger Mountain

Stranger on the trail: “It’s a survival day.”

I soloed Tiger tonight, and it became one of those interesting rides. As in “May you live in interesting times” (also wikipedia).

The Seattle weather deployed it’s full fickleness tonight. It was sunny in the parking lot, but clouded over a little as I climbed the fire-road. The conditions looked good – after a few hot dry weeks there had been a few days of rain and there was a nice sticky dampness on underlying hard ground.

Even as I reached Preston the rain started to fall and was soon a proverbial waterfall. It was as wet as I’ve ever seen it. The type of wet that requires four days of written notification of a desire to slow down and possibly even stop with V-brakes. This wasn’t helped at all by the fact that even though I learned from last week and was wearing my orange-lensed Smith’s it was still pretty dark to see. And then the rain started to blot out even that view. Taking my glasses off quickly let to a glob of mud in my contact lens eye, so I had to put them back on until finally I had to ride without them.

Within the first mile of Preston it became the type of ride where you kept your wits, kept in control and hoped you didn’t get a mechanical.

It was about then that my rear derailleur cable snapped, leaving me to single speed it for the rest of the ride in the smallest cog. This led to much uphill mashing and walking and going downhill on Preston and NWTT much too fast. Remember the part about not being able to stop or see? There were a few exciting corners, and riding down in that much flooding water makes a kayak seem preferable at times.

I met a couple of other riders who were also in different stages of dampness (all rain gear leaks in weather like this, my older jacket gave up after 5 minutes) and we leapfrogged our way around the trail as I alternatively walked, ran, flew and swam. No major injuries and home to some India Bistro leftovers.

All in all a grand night – if in the perverse mountain biker mould.
