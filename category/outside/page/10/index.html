Ride: Devil’s Gulch and Mission Ridge

Hot, hot, hot!

I think I almost killed my two riding buddies.

Morgan and Forest didn’t have the benefit of post-half-marthon legs even though it was the longest ride for me since last year (Tigers not withstanding).

Splash!

Morgan and Forest had responded to an email that looked like this:

It struck me this weekend that summer is here.

One of the great opportunities of the Northwest is to undertake “epic” mountain bike rides.

The type of rides that you have to carpool 3-hours in each direction to get to. They type of rides that sometimes have you wondering whether you need two bladders for your camelback in the desert. The type of rides that make large $7 safeway po’boys the food of choice. The hot sunny days that make the mental image of a Corona the only reason to keep on pedaling.

These rides are cross-country 10-25 milers (!) basically singletrack, often with hellacious climbs, and generally spectacular views and/or riding.

With that in mind I’d like to try and organize the first of the epic rides of the summer: Devil’s Gulch and Mission Ridge (out by Cashmere/Wenatchee)…

And so we ended up riding the Gulch/Ridge combo on Sunday. It still remains one of my favourite rides, even though the guys and I suffered a little.

It was high 80s but not humid and I was working off of reasonably strong legs, which my compadres were not. Combined with this being my sixth (or somesuch) time on this trail I was in front most of the way, leading to a few scares and at least one rather painful random tumble into the undergrowth (on a slow uphill section, sad).

We started at noon, climbed the Gulch, rested and had lunch in the meadow at the Mission Ridge junction (Safeway fatboys to the rescue) and finished up a little before six, 18 miles the wiser, after descending the Ridge.

We drove to Cle Elum and had steak and chips in the Caboose Bar and Grill before rolling back into Seattle in the dark. Very late, with several blown return times for our respective partners.

Next time we need to ride faster.

Forest in a Forest

Anyone signing up for the next ride -> email me.

Running Boy

Sunday, 9th July, Bellevue, Seafair Half-Marathon
1 Hour 42 Minutes 21 Seconds (unofficial)

A few weeks of preparation paid off in the run yesterday.

I was pretty happy with my time – over 6 minutes better than the struggle from the year before (although eating gourmet food the night before had been fun). Surprisingly many of the elements of the run were the same: critical toilet break at first Honey Buckets, shin splints at 2-4 miles, a pretty slow first half (working the pacer at 8 min/miles up to the 5-mile mark) and some exceptionally nice running between 7 and 12 miles.

If I could work out how to run the same way as I feel at about 10 miles, I could even have a future in the game. As it was I got a little worried at hot knees in the last mile, but sneaked it in regarldless, dropping runners for the last few miles, even up to the last .1 (of 13.1) mile stretch.

Before cockiness sets in though, Danika said the tannoy had the first half-marathoner coming in at 1 hour 7 minutes – that’s better than a 5 minute almost a 4-minute mile. For 13.1 miles.

Ride: Inspiring Tiger

Somewhere between watching Wimbledon and fetching a glass of water I found myself loading the Trek on the car and heading to Tiger today.

I’ve not been mountain biking recently – training for the half marathon, a few cancelled plans, doing random time-suck things, social activities at work and a weekend of weddings – and I had promised myself a decadent lazy 4th of July long weekend. Apart from doing wedding things that had been working well – it’s high 70’s, blue skies and I had a mammoth of a book to slog through (although I quite enjoyed it in the end). Sitting in the back yard with the occasional cold beverage was portraying feelings of beach and Mexico, all without leaving the home.

However I think I must have been grouching a little about mountain biking and found myself in that “it’s a little too much effort” zone. With some subtle tweaking (I’m sure) Danika portrayed such indifference that I headed off to Tiger to kill some time rather than not, conveniently ignoring that this might not be the sort of biking most conducive to pre-marathon training. Oh yeah, and it was a little cloudy and rainy, perhaps even low-70’s this morning.

Tiger itself was, in short, inspiring. The kit and bike was ready without effort due to a cancelled post-work ride from last week: I pulled on my Sugoi arms because it was a little nippy and headed up the fireroad only for the last mile to be in blazing sunshine as the clouds burned off. However it wasn’t too hot, and I nipped down Preston and then NWTT in good time. Nary a foot wrong, many bikers and hikers out on the trails. Dry, a lttile dusty, with great speed potential. I had forgotten how much fun this was. I had forgotten. Mmm, maybe it’s time to start arranging some summer epic rides too, as I’ve probably forgotten how much fun those are too.

That way I might also ride something other than Tiger this year.
