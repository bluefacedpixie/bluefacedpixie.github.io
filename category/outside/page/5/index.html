Ride: Back in the saddle

I spent an hour on Saturday cleaning mould from Camelbak hoses as the girls slept in the guest room – q-tips, listerine and coat hangars worked wonders.

It’s a small thing, but biking season is back!.

Today, Forest and I hit Tiger for the first time since October. He was fast, but we were both slow. I was happy I managed to pull my larger-self up the fireroad without any embarrassment beyond an utter reliance on granny gear. Preston was in good conditionand NWTT has some scatterings of fallen brush.

Rolling time was 1hr 36min, which is fine in one respect, and borderline disgraceful in another. The fireroad was 37.

Plenty of time to improve.

2006 Activity Zeitgeist

Similar to last year’s What did you do in 2005? here is my compilation from Excel of my activities in 2006.

Many of you will be glad to see that I did nothing on 153 days – almost half the year! I did approximately 372 activities, which means I double-dipped on only 7 days, which is pretty low (i.e. I basically never did a bike to work *and* something else). I’ve made a funky little pie chart showing the overview:

I would like to make a claim that I didn’t log a long summer and autumn of doing regular evening “pregnancy walks” with Danika, but really, they didn’t count anyway.

Side notes: I only went to the gym once (for ski conditioning), our hiking was mostly compressed into August (getting ready for vacation) and then September (the vacation), 11 out of my 12 mountain bike rides were at Tiger, and for the first time I snowboarded the same amount as a I mountain biked. Justifying not buying a new mountain bike but the new on-sale snowboarding kit quite nicely. Apart from gym and basketball all of my activities were outside which I like.

In terms of the new bike (Redline 925) I rode it 48 times compared to the Volpe’s 76, but 44 of the Volpe’s rides had been before I had the 925. So since purchase the rides have been 48 vs. 32. Redline 925 wins.

Finally, moving job seriously reduced my work miles on the bike: from 16 to 9 a day, and I really feel the lack of a critical mass in the shorter commute.

7:56 am to 4:20 pm

It’s the shortest day in Seattle (or was that yesterday?). Anyway, close enough – grey, overcast, damp, last day at work (with official blessing to skive off early). It must be Christmas.

Danika is weather and fact obsessed so informed me this morning that we had 8 hours of sun/daylight today and reeled off the sunrise and sunset times. I was shocked, but a quick check at weather.com did confirm that Seattle sunrise is 7:56 am and sunset today is 4:20 pm. So a little more than 8 hours. But not much.

Glasgow, on the other hand, is 8:45 am to 3:46 pm. I knew I liked Seattle’s weather better!

Bikes!

It must be something about pending parenthood. Not only am I now an active member of the community with the park; I attended a public meeting on the Seattle Bike Master Plan tonight, along with hundreds of others. I didn’t even learn about the meeting until I noticed a sign on my ride in to work this morning.

Highlights: I’m not the only one who hates the exit from the west side of Ballard Bridge heading south – Morgan gave me a reprimand for “graffiti” on the comments poster for phrases such as “fix the death trap”, but in the Q&A someone else said “fix it before we take sledgehammers to it”. Justice, sweet justice.

Lowlights: It’s trying to be all things to all people – trails for the Sunday families who like trails, lanes for the cyclists who are scared of traffic, and shared signs on arterials for riders who believe proactive aggressive is the best defense. They won’t all be implemented fully, so I’m sure we;’ll continue with a solution that isn’t properly joined up. But then, I’m Scottish cynical.
