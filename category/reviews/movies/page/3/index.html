The Flying Scotsman

The Flying Scotsman (3/5) – Good solid little movie from the UK (with EU financing).

First came to my attention as an airplane movie in August but picked it up in the New Releases at the video store last night. The movie is pretty much what it says on the tin – a story of the adventures of Graeme Obree, a Scotsman, who made his own bike for about Â£50 and went on to beat several world records in the early 90’s.

Good, if not Oscar, performances from Johnny Lee Millar, Billy Boyd and Brian Cox. The female cast didn’t show strong, especially the wife, 50% of whose lines were (literally) “Come on, Graeme!”.

Movie: The Promise

We’re currently in the ‘long wait’ phase of pregnancy prior to ‘the arrival’. And we’ve started to watch a lot of movies. I haven’t posted my mini-reviews for a while but thought I would attempt to kick them off once more.

The Promise is an epic (but short) colorful Chinese hero-movie. Which I liked. 4/5 – if you don’t like this genre go with a safer 3/5 but I just can’t bring myself to think the movie isn’t a little better than that.

It’s best watched in Chinese with English subtitles (a fact confirmed when we accidentally started watching it in dubbed English). The story is a little hokey – schoolboy fantasy maps with a superhero-esque race in the mountains, true love, appearances by goddesses, a nice bad-guy. Very satisfying.

Similar to many movies of its ilk the joy is in suspending a lot of disbelief, especially around the fight sequences, and revelling in the flow of the photography and the gorgeous colours and action.

CGI is a little off in the movie, especially in the beginning where there are a lot things attempted that don’t work, followed by a big battle scene that uses the cheap version of whatever software was used in Two Towers and Return of the King and includes the “all falling down” effect from Fellowship of the Rings. There, I think I successfully managed to refer to all of The Lord of the Rings with that sentence.

Movie: Superman Returns

The best thing I can say about this is that it is soo much better than The Producers (1/5) which was our primary movie of the night (Danika didn’t want to watch Superman so it was relegated to the “rip it for later or stay up late and watch it” category). But Producers sucked soo much ass we bailed in under an hour (a blessing in a 2+ hour movie) and I cranked up Superman (another 2+ hour movie).

It didn’t hold Danika for long and it barely held me. If someone had called to go to the pub or have an earwax removal I would have considered it. It was slow, it was enjoyable, it didn’t grab, it looked airbrushed, it was pleasant. You know that someone isn’t exciting when they’re called nice. I can’t say much more without giving away the plot but if you’ve read any magazines or trailers then they probably did that for you anyway. All I can say is that the overriding production values for this movie were “don’t blow it”.

Spacey as Lex Luthor is old school cameo – wasted talent.

I’m holding out for Spiderman 3.

So: Superman Returns (2/5) (scoring here)

Movie: Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man’s Chest

Ugh

Ok, I’ll get it over with: *1/5* (run away).

I should have known better – I didn’t like Pirates I and generally think Depp is a bit of a w**ker (sorry Susan), although I do like Keira, Orlando isn’t bad (Kingdom of Heaven was pretty good even) and I’m a sucker for a good swordfight.

Everyone else loves Pirates though (even Danika thought it was ok), so I thought I was the odd one out. And Pirates II had been #1 in the US for three weeks, we were walking around Ballard Seafood Fest in the hot, hot heat, and the air-conditioned matinee called out to me. And Danika had mentioned that she would like to see it (I would liek to state that this is _so_ not her fault though). So in we went.

It was pretty good. At the start. Great intro photography, quick escalation of conflict with Captain Jack. Then it got a little dull. Then slow. Then both Danika and I realized the movie was only half-done (it’s *143* minutes).

There were some great set pieces, and apparently for the sequel everyone got a clause in their contract that they were allowed to overact as much as Depp did in Pirates I. Except for Keira, who was a little bit of a wooden board. Even the _big_ swordfight ended in a kinda ridiculous “overstunt” involving a waterwheel. Really.

Bill Nighy was Davy Jones, but under so much make-up that at times I thought he was James Cosmo, and he always is a little over the top anyway (ever since his almost-screen-debut in Love Actually) so he gets a by.

The highlights?

  1. They never say (far less mispronounce) Caribbean that I noticed – as in “carry”-“be”-“ann” and not “car”-“rib”-“eh”-“inn”.
  2. They have an evil cliffhanger, that’s really good because it means the pain is over (at this stage you think the movie has just fed you another false summit and is going to go on for more).
  3. It actually makes Heathrow seem attractive and teary-eyed. Oh, wait, that’s Love Actually.

Movie: Aeon Flux

Aeon Flux 2/5.

I knew nothing about this movie except a super-cool trailer I saw months ago. Then I thought “sci-fi, Charlize Theron, probably bad but eye candy, watch the video”. So last week I did. Watch the video that is. Although I had briefly flitted with the idea of even watching it on the big screen. Had I known, however, that it was a cartoon adaption from the 90’s (MTV apparently) I would have reconsidered. Ouchee ouch.

So far the X I, II, hopefully III and Spiderman I & II are the only movies in recent history to pull off this type of movie, and that’s not even sci-fi – it’s superhero.

I’m just going to spoil it for everyone. If you watch it, you won’t really care.

Charlize is Aeon, a super cool fighting hottie that happens to be the rebirth of the tyrant’s beloved (who wasn’t a super cool fighting machine but some sort of pseudo-Irish dark haired girl). She stumbles into her arch-enemy (the tyrant) while trying to assassinate him (‘coz she thinks he killed her sister). But it’s ok! He’s a goodie, his brother is bad and they’re long lost lovers! How convenient. But wait, there is more. He has found the cure to global infertility (oh yeah, the world is only 5 million people on an idyllic city but it looks nice and forested outside, and I’ve no idea what happened to Portugal). Except his brother wants the cure suppressed (thus killing people e.g. Aeon’s sister) as he likes the brave new world of tube-birth. Bad. But it’s ok, because people are curing themselves naturally, so the whole storyline is pointless. Good. And then Charlize has to destroy the cloning factory, which means if they’re wrong, eveyone will die. Good? Restoring death to the world, it’s actually a pretty neat twist.

Oh, and they also break down the protective wall so the nice outside jungle can get in. Because it wouldn’t be Logan’s Run, Planet of the Apes sci-fi without the save the world part.

It looks good, but it’s as bad as The Island (1/5), but gets a higher rating due to lack of product placement. It also has a few main female characters, which is good for a movie of this type, so it earns a little street-cred points too.
