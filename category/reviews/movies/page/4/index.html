Movie: Match Point

Match Point: 2/5

Woody Allen fails to convince. A pseudo-thriller aka The Talented Mr. Ripley (a 3 or 4/5) but without the character conviction.

Scarlett was very hot, and the guy (Jonathan Rhys Meyers) very sleazy – I’m not sure why anyone would like him. It was unclear whether he represented the hero or anti-hero, but you didn’t really care, either way.

The movie was too long, and flat, and the ending felt a little like one that had screentested well with viewers of low intelligence.

The worst scene goes to poor James Nesbitt who played a detective (despite doing a better job on TV) and had a verbose monologue that felt like 3rd year high school drama.

Wait to see it on the plane.

Movie: Mission Impossible 3

Mission Impossible 3: 3/5

A solid movie – trying to combine the complexity of Mission Impossible with the Woo-ness of MI2 and exceeding at neither. However there were solid stunts, kind-of a fun plot, some crappy “I look like Katie Holmes” casting and it passed the critical action movie test – it was over faster than expected and I didn’t look at my watch.

The failing of the movies continue to be the move to make Tom Cruise aka Ethan Hunt the main focus of the stories with little team play. It puts the movie straight into the role of solo action movie aka James Bond without the character development, franchise or panache. Although it also has a great theme rift.

Enjoy, have fun, don’t expect prizes.

Movie: Vendetta

V for Vendetta, 4/5.

Caught this movie during the week and really liked it. Natalie Portman is excellent, and “V” is suitably freaky with his mask. I read the novel a long time ago, so I didn’t really notice anything too jarring about the screenplay (I think it changed from tyranny vs. anarchy to tyranny vs. pseudo-democracy in it’s message) although the graphic novel writer (Allan Moore) withheld his name from the credits. But he does that will all his movies – just as well considering one of them was the awful League of Extraordinary Gentlemen 2/5.

So that I don’t spoil it – it’s a dark near-future story set in Britain. There is a tyranny of fear and one man stands against the system, inspired by Guy Fawkes and the 5th of November (with suitable license on historical accuracy). Much mumbo-jumbo, action and love-story commences with many corny psuedo-American liberal hyperbole, but the accents are pretty decent.

Watch, rent, maybe even buy it.

Movie: Hustle and Flow

Misogynist rantings from the underside of life in Memphis to a beating Rap/Hip-Hop (I can never tell) soundtrack.

I rented this at the weekend in a post-Oscar impulse and because Danika likes the more polite H-H. We expected little.

It was actually a really fun movie, I have little problem reccomending it to any adults in the audience and it beats 8 Mile hands down as far as a low-life trying to make it through music goes. I also found some parallels to Laurel Canyon, but this was more real in it’s this-is-just-what-MTV-wants-you-to-think hip way.

A surprising 3/5 for Hustle and Flow.

Movie: Transporter 2

Dialogue that makes you want to weep.

Stunts that are so unbelievable that the suspension of disbelief has to be suspended.

The phrase “bollocks” used many times by the audience.

However, we just watched the DVD because I was allowed to rent an action movie. The storyline was hokey and any of the finesse of The Transporter was lost in relocation to Miami, Florida. However there were some of the coolest stunts* I have ever seen in this movie – and the really unbelievable ones were cool too, but mostly silly** – although I don’t watch that many action movies these days (apparently XXX is madness too). The movie varies between a 1 for corny and a 4 for coolness. I can’t really say anymore about the movie because there is really only a basic two-themed story (Man on Fire bleeds into something more) and one subplot (apparently the Mom is hot) and even describing it gives too much away; but really it’s all about Jason doing his own stuff while wearing a Blues Brothers suit.

Transporter 2: 3/5

(*) jumping between two cars hitting each other head on and then landing on the crashed bonnets/hoods

(**) really too many to count, but how about a fist fight on a flat-spinning Lear jet?

Movie: War Of The Worlds (2005)

I was banned from going to see this in the movies in the summer due to Tom’s sofa-jumping antics on primetime US TV. And I think that was fair.

However, I’ve just finished watching the DVD and I was quite pleasantly surprised. Generally my attitude to both blockbuster movies and sci-fi movies is the same: expect the worst and you will often not be disappointed. So in this case I went in expecting a 2 and thought it was significantly better.

Despite a last minute burst of saccharin the modern setting of the classic “Tripods massacre of the human race” set in a contemporary world of divorce, shared children, America the greatest and “is it the terrorists” actually played out quite well. Although I think the Babylon 5 rip of burying the machines many years ago and using lightning to implant the pilots was a bit of psuedo-explanation too far. But I was reared on Tolkien and expect to suspend belief without Star Trek explanations.

I’ll spoil it a little too much if I write more, but here is a last pro and con. Pro: Tim Robbins is much better than the calvary (or was it artillery?) man from Jeff Wayne’s version. Con: Jeff’s had better music.

So overall a 3/5, and a fond memory of Primary 6 in Kilsyth Primary when on Friday afternoon we would get an hour of talking books, which introduced me to The Hobbit, and the War of the Worlds.
