2013 Zeitgeist

A somewhat random, mini-zeitgeist of interesting activity based on my 2013 spreadsheet. 2007 was the last time I did anything like this.

So, 2013… numbers approximate…

  • 173 days where I could commute to work (not sick, on holiday, traveling for work etc)
  • 155 of them I did commute on bike at least one way (almost all of them both ways) that’s almost 90% of the days (I’m almost surprised its not more…)
    • 94 of these on the Volpe, 61 on the 925
    • 38 of them were wet at least one way
  • I ran 44 times
  • I mountain biked a staggering 30 times (which for a father who doesn’t get out much and busted himself for two months is pretty good) – thank you Ecuador!
  • I boarded 12 times, mostly thanks to taking Lucy to ski lessons and killing time at Alpental
  • I swam about half a dozen times

All in all about 246 days of activity – I was aiming for 250 but frankly I’m happy, happy (and had such a lazy/inconvenient December) especially as this accommodated about 8 weeks business travel.

Now, about the mountain biking total for 2014 (and the New York Marathon 2014)….

2007 Activity Zeitgeist

Well its that time again where I’ve frittered away a couple of hours analyzing my activities log from last year.

A change in format of the Excel spreadsheet was not that useful, and it’s actually taken longer to pull together the raw numbers than last year; and then it got a little too interesting when I could go all anal over comparing the numbers to 2006 and 2005.

In preparation – this year sees a significant decrease in activities overall. My spreadsheet has no entries for 92 days from Lucy’s birth to Danika’s return to work (and I need to take the bike). Some points of embarrassment:

2006 Activity Zeitgeist

Similar to last year’s What did you do in 2005? here is my compilation from Excel of my activities in 2006.

Many of you will be glad to see that I did nothing on 153 days – almost half the year! I did approximately 372 activities, which means I double-dipped on only 7 days, which is pretty low (i.e. I basically never did a bike to work *and* something else). I’ve made a funky little pie chart showing the overview:

I would like to make a claim that I didn’t log a long summer and autumn of doing regular evening “pregnancy walks” with Danika, but really, they didn’t count anyway.

Side notes: I only went to the gym once (for ski conditioning), our hiking was mostly compressed into August (getting ready for vacation) and then September (the vacation), 11 out of my 12 mountain bike rides were at Tiger, and for the first time I snowboarded the same amount as a I mountain biked. Justifying not buying a new mountain bike but the new on-sale snowboarding kit quite nicely. Apart from gym and basketball all of my activities were outside which I like.

In terms of the new bike (Redline 925) I rode it 48 times compared to the Volpe’s 76, but 44 of the Volpe’s rides had been before I had the 925. So since purchase the rides have been 48 vs. 32. Redline 925 wins.

Finally, moving job seriously reduced my work miles on the bike: from 16 to 9 a day, and I really feel the lack of a critical mass in the shorter commute.
