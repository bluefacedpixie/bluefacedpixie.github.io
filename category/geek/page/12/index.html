12th Mar 06: Ugly Big Switch

12th Mar 06: Ugly Big Switch, originally uploaded by machaggis.

Since we moved to our CD-only Forester in 2004, there has been major mobile sound issues in the Waddell household. We’d become used to our infinite selection of music from our iPod playing through our cassette adapter in many cars – the Beetle and (almost) all of the cars we used in our trip around the world. Indeed, it can be said that having a large selection of music for a year adrift in Le Grande Tour may have avoided many issues and contributed to our marriage.
But we’ve struggled with non-cassette technology – the iTrip and the other FM transmitter devices (Belkin etc) have sapped our wallets and my will to live. After a particularly annoying drive to Whistler last week I finally cracked. Yesterday I went to Car Toys (ugh!) and bought and got them to install an “FM modulator”. Basically it takes over the radio (and cuts out the antenna) so that you can play your iPod (or other device) directly through the stereo. It, of course, cost like 10 cents and various charges like installation and “6 feet of cable” to bring the price to a gazillion dollars. Which I had been assiduously avoiding. But we used it today in our <200 mile round trip to Stevens Pass and it performs as advertised, so to that regard I’m happy (I only have the CD-only Miata to sort out now).
However, the undisclosed downside of the Car Toys experience is that you have to throw a switch to disable the radio and enable the iPod. And this switch, although it nestles in a blank Subaru switch beside the steering wheel, is ugly, and red. I’m still in shock – but it made A Photo Per Day!

The end of freaky-blinky

I took the trek to the Apple Store last night with my flaking power supply for my PB and my freaky-blinky iPod shuffle.

For the AppleCare’d power supply the Genius Bar spent 45 minutes testing it, blaming the powerbook and then looking for a replacement part (which they had to order and I have to go pick up).

For the 14-month old (i.e. out of warranty) shuffle all they did was listen to me, plug it into their machine to make sure I wasn’t lying, looked at a tech support page and then gave me a new one. Problem solved, and clearly it’s not unique. I used to have sentimental attachment to objects but as the “replace it, it’s a consumer device” culture continues I’m starting to get over it. Bye Bye old shuffle, hello new shuffle.

And it was sad to see that my $149 shuffle has a replacement cost of $89. That’s depreciation, even though the price changes were to make space for the $149 1Gb Nano.

Random Website Facts (January 06)

One of the best things about doing the websites the almost-hard-way (running a site, remote hosting – as opposed to self-hosting which is the really-hard-way) is that you can occasionally browse the pretty pictures that they (the hosting company aka luxsci) provide for usage patterns in terms of page hits and Kbytes transferred etc. (Yawn).

I spent a few minutes doing this tonight, but want to draw attention to my favourite section: search terms that hit the website (this includes the three main blogs – bfp, pd, pm – and the main machaggis.net website).

So you can tell many things from this list:

  • danika is the favourite search term
  • we don’t exactly solicit search engine placement: danika is only found twice in January, three times if you count danika marschner and we only got 17 matches overall!
  • someone searching for crazy wedding dancing found us
  • we’ve historically always had occasional hits for the Ivy Guest House in Siem Reap in several formats (see our world travels)
  • I also don’t know why we match to pink canoes and gaitano’s new jersey, think of it as ethnic diversity

And if you’re interested it looks like on Windows derivatives is the favourite browser with bloglines (how-to) second and Firefox-derivates on the Mac third (which is probably me!).

Oh yeah, and bluefacedpixie has more hits than planetdanika. But then, it’s almost midnight, I’m geeking out and Danika is in bed.
