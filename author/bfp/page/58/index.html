Strange Rant Regarding American Cycling

“Cycling just became weirder and more off-putting than beauty pageants for 7-year-old girls.”

The Dark Side Of American Cycling Is On Display Now from AOL Sports Blog (kinda a random link):

A bitter and jealous old champion who has never received the adulation he feels he deserves, a dominant personality with a carefully protected image who is fighting to keep his name out of the mud that the rest of the cycling world is stained with, an Olympic champion who appears to have been a total fraud and an ornery Mennonite who’s business manager is pitifully trying to blackmail the bitter old champion with his childhood trauma. You couldn’t write a soap opera with this many people who can’t stand each other and with this many axes to grind. And this is American cycling. On the bright side, it’s less screwed up than European cycling, if you can believe that.

We are the mass extinction event

We are the mass extinction event, originally uploaded by machaggis.

In terms of geological time, we are still but a small pimple on the face of the planet.
Yet we probably are as damaging as the last big meteor.
As a case in point – the elephant-shaped hedges advertising “Bear Week” at the entrance to the San Diego Zoo have what appear to be ivory tusks.
From the ever-dubious-but-mostly-correct wikipedia on the Elephant:

Elephants are increasingly threatened by human intrusion. Between 1970 and 1989, the African elephant population plunged from 1.3 million to about 600,000; the current population is estimated to be between 400,000 and 660,000.

This is from a zoo which is dedicated to conservation. I quite enjoyed the fur coats they sold in the gift shop.

Work or Play?

Polar Bear Cub 2, originally uploaded by machaggis.

Last week I found myself with a handful of hours in the middle of a San Diego day. I had a computer, I had internet, I could do work, I had more than a few personal chores (*cough* photo organization *cough* bloggin *cough*) that I could do.
But I found myself opting for that travel itch, of a touristy variety.
I headed to the San Diego Zoo for a few hours, an encore visit to one of my first big “American” experiences – in 1995 (or ’96?).
It was fun, hot and a good trip. Less fazed with the Americana of it all now (sodas really can be that size, water can be that dear) I found the zoo itself less expansive and wonderful than before. The cages seem smaller, the animals more sedate. However it is still one of the best zoos I have ever seen, and this time I did get to see the polar bears.
With Lucy aging by the minute, our family membership to the Woodland Park Zoo awaits – so I better get used to this sort of thing.
