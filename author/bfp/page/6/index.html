17th November 2002 – An anonymous Lisu Village, North Thailand

A little delay in updating the retrospective travel log since the start of November, and we’ve moved continent only to get stuck in one country – Thailand! Almost 2 weeks in Thailand since we headed there from Paris (at this time Thailand was our longest “one-country” experience) and we basically did a little Bangkok (which we returned to as it was in the centre of the country), went to beach-land in Koh Samui (only for the weather to not play nice) and then head north to Chang Mai (which we loved). We experienced hardcore overnight Thai tourist buses (back to back from the Samui to Bangkok to Chang Mai at one stage) and stayed in some sketchy, sketchy, cheap ass accomodation. In all, the log is best left as a stimulus to the imagination.

What Wat, Watt? – 16th November 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Smile Internet and Massage Parlour, Chiang Mai, Thailand]

Wow! The last 12 days have been both eventful and relaxing. Filled with hot chilis, steamy nights and Thai massages. Lazy days in a beach resort followed by the relative chaos of Bangkok. Multiple overnight bus trips, NO museums or cathedrals (gasp!) and semi-luxury airline travel (bigger gasp!). It’s been an up and down ride, but we are loving Thailand!

When we last wrote we were off to the Eiffel Tower in Paris. That was a really good excursion but completely unlike our experiences so far in Thailand. Our flight to Thailand from Paris was great because we managed to get Business Class upgrades instead of flying cattle class. This was an unexpected treat – good food, good movies (we even managed to finally get to see ‘Road to Perdition’ which we had chased across the US and Europe) and we arrived in Bangkok pretty happy and alert to sunshine!

We more or less immediately headed off to the beaches in South Thailand, but our planning went a little awry. In deference to our Seattle roots we managed to arrive on the exotic island of Koh Samui in the midst of monsoon season. The Lonely Planet clearly states that the rainy season ends in October, whereas it starts in November! Regardless we chilled for a few days, using slightly nicer hotel rooms than if we had spent all our time on the beach. We spent one day on a wild safari including several hours of terrifying off-roading and some excellent scenery. The rest of our time was spent avoiding the rain – eating yummy food, watching MTV, getting foot massages and reading our books.

Rather than chase the sun to the west coast (as all the other tourists were) we decided that we would leave South Thailand for now and return later. So we headed back to Bangkok again, and only stopped there for a few hours before coming to Chiang Mai. This ended up being quite an uncomfortable journey! Excited at the prospect of disgustingly cheap bus travel in Thailand (5 pounds or 8 dollars for bus & boat from Koh Samui to Bangkok for instance) we took two overnight buses back to back. Ugh. The first one was ‘ok’ but the second was really run down and parts of it literally broke!

Nevertheless, we made it safely to Chiang Mai and we like it – much more manageable than Bangkok. So far our time here has been spent doing touristy things – we went to see several Wats (Thai/Buddhist temples) and did a Thai Cookery course. We learned how to make curry paste which involved lots of pounding with a mortar and pestle and some wayward chilis in Danika’s eye – ouch! We’re hoping to be able to replicate these skills outside the comfortable kitchen of our Thai teacher.

Tomorrow we’re going to go trekking for 3 days in the jungle. This involves covering ourselves in insect repellent, walking up and down hills, meeting hill tribes, elephant rides and river rafting!

Not all our activities in Chiang Mai have been pleasurable however. We discovered that we had some cash and a credit card stolen – sometime between Koh Samui and the bus to Chiang Mai we think – and had to spend some time reporting this to the police and the credit card company. We still have to do some paperwork over the next few weeks but apart from that it’s mostly resolved.

We’ve done other things in Thailand. We’ve gone to markets (where we still haven’t spent a disgraceful amount of money), sweated a lot, eaten lots of Thai food and been to Starbucks in Koh Samui and Chiang Mai.

Gotta go practise our elephant riding skills…

Danika and Gordon

5th November 2002 – Overnight Paris to Bangkok; Thai Airways

It’s Guy Fawkes Day, and this time ten years ago we were overnighting from Paris to Bangkok and had somehow scored a business class seat on our cheap tickets (something to do with the strangeness of my clear expired British Midland Diamond Club card, or sheer luck that we checked in at the right time, at the right desk). And thus our European episode of the great world travels drew to an end.

We closed in style, Vatican and a great visit to Paris (where we returned several times subsequently) hitting every museum in site including Versailles and the Eiffel Tower. And we were done. And ready for some sun (!?), and a stretch in one country for more than a handful of days.

Ooh la la! – 4th November 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[easyInternet, Paris, France]

Today is our last full day in Europe. This is the most difficult newsletter to write to date because we have to write it on a French keyboard!!

Our last days in Rome saw us incresingly frustrated with various ‘Italianisms’ and we were more than happy to arrive in Paris (at last the toilets have seats again!). We have been in Paris for a few days now. We’re living near the Pigalle area, which is the red light district but is in reality little more than a sad tourist trap.

We’ve been sightseeing like crazy; we bought a museum pass which gets us into almost any sight in Paris (except the Eiffel Tower), and lets you skip most of the lines, and in some cicumstances even lets you avoid the metal detectors (although we’re not too sure this is really meant to be a feature). With the pass we’ve seen numerous museums such as the Louvre, Orsay, Picasso, Rodin and Pompidou, gone up the Arc de Triomphe and picnicked at Versailles.

We have finally completed our quest to eat our way across Europe, and our waistlines are currently showing the result. Our most memorable meal of late was at a bizarre fondue restaurant where wine came in baby bottles, complete with nipples! Foods that we’ll miss the most: European chocolate, gelato, tapas, French cheese, LOTS of pizza and the odd glass of wine.

Tomorrow we fly to Thailand where the plan is to go straight to the beach (although it will probably take a few days and adventures to get there from Bangkok). We’ve packed our bags, done our laundry and put our bikinis, shorts and t-shirts at the top – ready to go! We still have a little time in Paris (it’s only the afternoon) and the only BIG sight that we have not done is the Eiffel Tower (can you believe that?) so that is on the cards for tonight.

With that in mind, we’ll head off into Paris once more with a fond ‘au revoir’.

Gordon & Danika.

29th October 2002 – Rome, Italy

Today we went to Pompeii, which was excellent, and we also had some wrestling with booking transit to Paris and ended up with a somewhat unplanned new plan to fly. Rome is pretty intense, and a little like London has a lot of “density” to it – not needing much area to cover a LOT of history and/or sights. We stayed in a hotel from Rick Steves’ which is where the train drivers on night duty sometimes stay, thankfully we were not asked to time-share our room during the day. It also had a TV – a rarity on our trip – and I distinctly remember watching Moby on it on MTV.

Did someone say pizza… – 28th October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Navigator 50 Cyber Cafe, Rome, Italy]

Buongiorno! It’s been too long since we wrote our last newsletter. We did try yesterday but the computer ate it, so we’re trying again today. Apart from that we have no real excuse – internet isn’t quite as easy/cheap in rural Italy, but mostly it was just because we’ve been too busy eating pizza, gelato and drinking cheap red wine.

Since Nice we’ve spent almost two weeks in Italy. We’ve done a whistlestop tour of many places, celebrated Danika’s 26th birthday in at least three different towns and arrived in Rome yesterday where we will settle for a few days.

So what have we seen and done??? Our Italian experience began with a mad 24 hours in Venice. We looked at the canals, took a gondola ride at night, ate Italian food, stayed in a nice hotel. It was really good, and the gondola ride was not as cheesy as we feared it might be, so we can recommend it to anyone as a once in a lifetime experience.

We continued on to Florence where we were based for a few days. We got Renaissance overload here, but thoroughly enjoyed it (the sights included the Duomo, David, Uffizi gallery, the Ponte Vecchio), and also went to Pisa on a day trip. The leaning tower was really cool, but Pisa was really *only* about the tower, so we didn’t do much else there (except eat pizza).

From Florence we toured Tuscany as best we could by train and bus and ended up going through Umbria. We went to Siena, San Gimignano, Assisi, Orvieto, Civita. Although we missed a lot of the less mainstream sights (and a lot of vineyards) by not having a car we managed to cram a fair amount in – next time a car is a must!

Out of all these towns we have a few selected memories for you:

Siena is the cutest town of our trip, Santa Fe loses big time. We stayed in a hotel run by Nuns with a view of the Duomo. We had bad colds but succeeded in eating well. By ordering things in Italian, we accidentally ended up with things such as liver, horse and lower bovine intestine but did escape the tripe! We also discovered one of our favorite Italian restaurants, serving dirt cheap food and wine as well as having really entertaining clientele.

San Gimignano has the most tourists, but we did get a great bottle of Chianti for about 4 bucks. Gordon had his favourite gelato here.

Civita is really hard to get to by bus. It took us almost all day to get there and back. But it was worth it. It’s a little pedestrian town reachable only by footbridge, and occupied by only 15 old Italian women. It isn’t even in the Lonely Planet guidebooks! Going to Civita was meant to be a birthday treat for Danika, but after all the fun with the buses, I think the better treat was the bottle of champagne in the hotel once we got back!

Now we are in Rome. Yesterday we toured the ruins of the ancient world (colosseum and roman forum) and although mostly rubble they were really interesting, big and fun. Gordon’s really glad he wasn’t a gladiator though.

Over the next few days we’ll do the rest of Rome, go to Pompeii on the Eurostar and then head to Paris. Then its Thailand and onwards.

Hope everyone is doing well and enjoyed their summer. Keep on writing as we’re getting further away as the days go by.

Ciao, G & D.

25th October 2002 – Orvieto, Italy

Getting to the end of our time in Italy we start focusing on small, somewhat famous medieval towns. We had the famous towers of San Gimignano, the pilgrimmage of Assisi and ancient Etruscan city of Orvieto. We also had an important lesson about being carless in Europe – when you rely on a funicular to take you to the town on top of the hill (from the town on the bottom of the hill) make sure you know when the last ride home is. I like the abbreviated note and will resolve how we were stranded on the next update.
