The Magic Yellow Line

I find it somewhat startling that I haven’t blogged about one of the coolest things in America before: the magic yellow line that is on televised American Football (*). When I first saw it on TV it was a truly miraculous sight, such ingenuity and blend of technology for purely entertainment purposes.

For those of you who are going eh?, the yellow line goes across the field in football and is the line that the football team gets a number of attempts to get the ball past – 10 yards from their last position. In real-life it is represented by pretty girls holding large red ice-cream cones on the sidelines while referees in striped black and white shirts run around.

So in honour of the last weekend’s Superbowl (Seattle weren’t in it this year) here is the technical explanation of how this little piece of wizardry works, courtesy of ars.technica.

Oh, and unlike last year Scotland didn’t do so well in their first Six Nations game.

(*) as opposed to fitba’ or soccer. i.e. real football.

Movie: Talladega Nights: The Ballad of Ricky Bobby

Given that new parent IQ drops directly in proportion to sleep deprivation et al, Danika and I rented some “low thought” Hollywood comedies on DVD. One, Scoop, turned out to be pretty fine (3/5) and I may write more about it. Talladega Nights, on the other hand, was gruesome, demeaning and just plain awful (1/5).

It says a lot about my current mental state that unlike our last Will Ferrell outing – Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy (*) – I actually ended up watching the whole thing, Danika was wiser and quit some 45 minutes in.

I really must learn I don’t like these movies, no matter how good the trailers are and how much other people seem to like them.

No links in this post, because I want to spare you the pain – but if you must buy it from Amazon, use the Ballard Corners Park link.

(*) Is there something about these movies where it’s Title: The story of somebody that is a clue? Anchorman is unrated because we turned it off – so (0/5)

Lucy Fanfare

Lucy Fanfare, originally uploaded by machaggis.

Considering I’m a new father, et al, I won’t elaborate on how many hours I’ve wasted today trying to get a rather geeky cool idea to work in terms of “introducing” Lucy with a little movie title sequence. Rather than, for example, sleeping when the baby is sleeping etc…
The Star Wars TSG program works fine (almost fast on the MacBook Pro) but it left me with 2817 still frame files that I singularly failed to manage (in my change-diaper, do chores, interrupt-driven manner) to do anything constructive with. Multiple attempts at iMovie (on a couple of computers) could not convince it to display frames at 25fps, and multiple attempts to export as a movie from iPhoto or import it as a iMovie slideshow all failed. The best iPhoto effort is a somewhat amusing slow-motion 45-minute sequence – the best iMovie equivalent is almost 8 hours long (glacial!). I did work out how to do it in iMovie, but it involved changing the time for each of the 2817 frames by hand (no bulk update) so I avoided that.
Maybe after this is posted someone will have produced an easy guide for how turn the frames into a movie on a Mac with no disk space, so that I can google it when I have time. Such as when Lucy is three.
