21st Mar 06: BalMar

21st Mar 06: BalMar, originally uploaded by machaggis.

It’s been a busy day in the Waddell house. Danika has had some excitement (slightly secret although I’m sure it will be public/planetdanika’d soon) and Morgan and I went to see V for Vendetta.

Who?

In the past couple of days I’ve discovered (re-discovered) the crusty old band “The Who“.

It started with the closing song of the 14th episode of House MD. Catchy, familiar, it turned out to be Baba O’Reilly (aka Teenage Wasteland) from The Who. When I went to download it though I also discovered that Who Are You is the theme to CSI, and Behind Blue Eyes is the “no one every thinks the bad guy has feelings” song that has been popping up on Seattle radio (the commercial stations, not KEXP) recently.

Feeling quite pleased with myself I downloaded a handful of songs from one of the greatest hits (My Generation: Very Best of The Who), only to discover that it’s a little too old-school rock for Danika. We have a large common ground, but I think this will be iPod-only for me until I manage to sneak the songs into a playlist when Danika isn’t expecting them.

19th Mar 06: Summer is coming

19th Mar 06: Summer is coming, originally uploaded by machaggis.

For various reasons I ended up close to the house today. It was a perfect spring day – blossoms, blue sky, frigid cold in the morning and out of the sun. I did yard work before lunch and then managed to convince myself that the “fix bikes” part of my to-do could be completed on the back patio. Regular maintenance for my commuter, a little bit of love for D’s rarely-used bike (in preparation for the Tour De Lopez – hopefully) and then I started to resurrect my mountain bike for the coming summer.
I think I may have the brakes working, I’ve cleaned off the congealed mud from 5/6 months in the basement and it’s pretty much ready to go. Although it’s starting to look like it needs another round of replacement parts (wheels, cables, brakes etc) I’m trying to not justify buying a new bike.
{D50, as taken}

Liberals being bred out of existence?

An excellent article on demographics on NPR’s weekend edition had me gripped as I drove to trail-running this morning. Apparently liberals have less children than more conservative family groups. So demographically they are slowly becoming extinct.

I was also amused by the terminology used for various groups of people.
