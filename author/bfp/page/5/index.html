26th December 2002 – Manly, Australia

Our first week in Australia captured the essence of the strangeness of this Land Down Under. We had a great time hanging out at the beach on Manly and exploring Sydney and had Christmas in the Sun! It did not feel right, but it felt *good*. We lived a little in luxury, using the apartment/house of Andy and Jane who were, unfortunately for us, Christmassing in England and, fortunately for us, had left their place empty and allowed us to use it! We would meet them later on the trip but the homely luxury of having a base that wasn’t a dodgy Thai lodge or a hostel was great and allowed us to cook and get our life in order. And, as with most of this trip, the Lord of the Rings movies dogged our footsteps and we went to see the opening day of The Two Towers on opening day in Sydney. I had read the book (again) using a large omnibus from Martin (and that Dublin visit oh-so-long-ago) that finally got traded in (at a poor return, that I still feel sore about ten years later) for other books at the 2nd hand bookstore in Manly.

Another day, another continent. – 24th December 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Andy & Jane’s Abode, Manly, Australia]

Merry Christmas everyone!

It seems strange to us, but we’re actually having Christmas in Australia at last! There’s a beach, there’s sun (a few clouds), there’s no rain or snow, and there is a general feeling of kicking back and having a good day. It’s feeling surreal that plans made in Seattle in the spring have finally come to fruition, but we’re loving it.

It’s noon in Australia, and so far we’ve been for a run along the promenade, had a champagne breakfast on the patio, went to the beach for a couple of hours and watched a Christmas Swimming Race. See below for a link to photos of how our Christmas Breakfast looked, and the beach at 9 am this morning: the Australians celebrate Christmas early!! We have plans to make a semi-traditional roast dinner today (ham), laze about in good Ozzie style and to phone home in the evening. For Boxing Day (Americans: the day after Christmas, also a holiday in the UK, Australia etc.) we’re going to a Barbeque lunch with an Australian friend of Gordon’s (met on his mountain bike trip in Peru) and then to see the local opening day showing of “The Two Towers”.

We’ve been in Australia for almost a week now, and we don’t really have much to say! We’re staying in Gordon’s friends apartment near Sydney at Manly Beach. This is a killer abode and is only missing two vital ingredients: the hosts! Andy and Jane are home in the UK for Christmas and New Year so we won’t get to see them until later in January 2003. However, we are conscientious house-sitters and are watering the plants as prescribed. We’re thoroughly enjoying domesticity after almost six months on the road, and have been mostly taking it easy, running, learning to boogie-board, cooking and drinking lots of wine (there is no wine in Asia so we’ve just had two months of beer-drinking).

Our sightseeing has been consumed by several attempts to do Christmas shopping in downtown Sydney; but we’ve managed numerous scenic ferry rides between Manly and Sydney, a walk around Darling Harbour and a short trip to the world famous Bondi Beach (which has no relationship to a certain Danika Bondi Marschner). We have some ideas about doing some more sightseeing in the next week or so before heading out of Sydney and up the coast for a little bit, but we haven’t made any firm plans yet.

Anyway, I think I hear some Christmas carols, and I’m sure it’s time for some sparkly wine, so all the best.

G’day cobbers,

Gordon & Danika

18th December 2002 – overnight Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia to Sydney, Australia

And so we, mostly, leave Asia. A small return is in our future and in a later time I will travel to Korea as if it was as nearby as Portland or Whistler, but for now we wind up our last week. We leave the delights of Ko Lanta, have our last dip in the swill and excitement of Bangkok (including chancing our luck one too many times with the overnight buses), a last aweseme Wat and Buddha, shopping and then Malaysia. A solid couple of days in Kaula Lumpur barely gave us a taste for it although we had some somewhat civilized experiences with the Petronas Towers and a Beethoven concert (I may have napped a little in comfort). And thus, onwards to Oz, following the yellow brick road.

“Smelly t-shirt, just 100 Baht! Toothpaste free!” – 16th December 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Excel Internet Cafe, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia]

Two long weeks since our last newsletter and we’ve done remarkably little in that time! More beach time, a couple of mad days in Bangkok and yesterday we flew to Kuala Lumpur (KL) in Malaysia for an extended stopover before Australia….

After several more beach days at Kata in Phuket, we took a boat to another island called Koh Lanta. Lanta is not as touristy as Phuket. Although we had less to keep us occupied in the evenings we didn’t notice the difference on the beach during our tanning time. While on Lanta we went to the Laanta Lanta local festival one night for some good food, fireworks and traditional performances. This helped us unwind from what had been a terrifying day on a motorbike touring the island. Gordon had demonstrated his developing motorbike skills with extreme driving maneuvers due to pothole-ridden roads, cobra infested pits, dirt tracks, vertical cliffs and an engine with enough power to work an electric toothbrush. All in all a fun day.

The vast majority of our days on Lanta were spent lounging on the beach with a little bit of snorkelling. We also managed to break out of our sedentary traveling mode and started running. So far we’ve been out a handful of times, mostly at sunset on the beach, and we’re feeling much the better for starting to work off some of our weight gain!

After five days on Koh Lanta, we took our last overnight bus to Bangkok. Unfortunately, the bus thieves struck and we lost such bizarre items as a tube of toothpaste, Gordon’s favorite t-shirt and Danika’s new running shorts. Nothing of value was taken but we had to spend a morning tracking down the police station and filing a report. Ugh. Our last day and a half in Thailand were spent in a shopping frenzy as we stocked up on family gifts and cheap cds.

In our first 24 hours in Malaysia we’ve been startled by the contrast between Kuala Lumpur and Bangkok. So far we love it here! It’s clean, modern, well-organized and potentially cheaper than Thailand. Today we went up the tallest buildings in the world (the Petronas Twin Towers), watched the new James Bond movie and had an excellent Indian dinner for $5. Tomorrow we’re even going to have Starbucks!

Our next missive should be from Australia before Christmas, so Best Wishes of the season to you all.

Until then,

Danika & Gordon

And we also crossed the bridge of turning the 6 month trip into something a little more. After Australia we would go to the temperate, Scottish-infused, Lord-Of-The-Rings-was-filmed-here location of New Zealand – pretty much where Cory and Laura are now, almost ten years later.

Aaaahh… sea, sand and surf. At last. – 3rd December 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Let’s Go Travel, Kata Beach, Phuket, Thailand]

We’ve finally ended up back on the beach in Thailand after starting with Koh Samui over four weeks ago. This time we’ve come to the south west of Thailand (the Andaman coast) and the weather has been really nice. So sunny that our plans to go beach hopping have slowed down considerably and we’ve now spent a few days in a nice secluded teak bungalow at Kata on Phuket. Kata is a ‘real’ tourist destination, not a backpacker place, so we’ve been enjoying the civilised comforts of beach chairs, Club Med beach bars, a generally better dress sense and more families than we’ve seen in a while. Unfortunately this is accompanied by some slightly more sharkish practises by the local Thais – taxis, phonecalls and food all cost significantly more than in the rest of Thailand – but we’ve not been caught out too badly now that we’ve been in the country for a few weeks.

Our last email left with us heading to Ayuthaya. We didn’t actually do that 🙂 Instead we went straight to Bangkok and organised flights to Siem Reap in Cambodia. This is the base town for travelling to see the Ankgors of Cambodia: Angkor Wat, Angkor Thom, Ta Promh…. (the list goes on). We had decided to fly as it was a long, hard and not very exciting alternative to travel to Siem Reap using a combination of bus, boat and truck. And although this would have been an adventure in itself we decided we’d like a little comfort and more time on Thailand’s beaches when we were finished.

Our short visit to Cambodia was excellent: we spent several days staying in a nice guesthouse and explored the temples and ruins by rented tuk-tuk (3 wheeled taxi motorbike) and moped. We then flew straight back to Bangkok and onwards to Phuket where we are now ensconced at Kata.

Phuket is quite civilised and on Sunday we went to Phuket Town and watched Harry Potter 2 at the movies. However because the tourist industry is a little depressed here we misjudged the ease of getting a taxi home after the show was over. However our day was saved by a friendly, Thai lady called Judy (who does graphic design with photoshop for German magazines!) who, along with her husband, took pity on us and gave us a ride home – over 20 km. This was one of the friendliest experiences we’ve had in Thailand and we were most grateful.

We hope all the Americans enjoyed Thanksgiving. Ours was spent at a pizza place in Cambodia where we splurged on a bottle of wine (wine is very expensive in Asia). We are feeling slightly homesick for Seattle this week as the Christmas Bash by superhost RoseMarie (accompanied by the esteemed Bob Hedricks) is on the 7th of December. We will have a fruity cocktail with an umbrella and straw in celebration. Merry early Christmas!

Finally, we have decided to take full advantage of our current employment status (i.e. unemployed wastrels) and extend our adventure. Instead of leaving Australia in January 2003 to return to sub zero temperatures (and many feet of snow) in Buffalo we’ve decided to go to New Zealand for several weeks to see where they made the Lord of the Rings movies. This should be quite good fun and we’re glad we don’t have to think about the end of our trip anytime soon. Although this means that Gordon misses the snowboard season in the US, he probably couldn’t afford to snowboard this year anyway! Plans for return are now early March 2003 – see you then.

Gordon and Danika

30th November 2002 – Kata, Phuket, Thailand

Five months or so into our travels it becomes apparent that we are not really slacking off and here we are in heart of beach-rich tourist-friendly Asia. Now admittedly our first Koh Samui island adventure was kinda “wet” but we decide to try again.

First of all we finish an awesome couple of days in Angkor Wat – sunrise on the second day (dark in the morning, and this is after sunset the previous day!) and then a little scooting about on a moped on the last day. Our last night in Cambodia was also Thanksgiving, so we had pizza (quintessentially as American as we could get and at least as good as Turkey) and some warm red wine. And then we returned to Thailand, baled straight out of Bangkok by flying (no more stinking buses) to Phuket and going to the decadently named Fantasy Hill. Where we did some beach.

When day 2 of the Phuket log simply states “Beach, pineapples” I think we can safely say we have temporarily taken our foot off the USA-England-Scotland-Ireland-Spain-France-Italy-France-Thailand-Cambodia-Thailand travel gas.

26th November 2002 – Siem Reap, Cambodia

A week. Floating light celebrations in Chang Mai. Going motorcycle riding around Sukhothai’s ruins (fastest way to get around when there isn’t landmines – see Cambodia) and it was fun! Now I can see why the trials riders all make great mountain bike riders but can’t cycle uphill. Then we had a big-bang-maybe-last-night in Bangkok (yes, we were back again) before heading to Cambodia solely to tour Angkor Wat (on the basis that every-year-there-is-less-of-it or its-more-commercialized or we-might-never-be-in-this-neck-of-the-woods-again or we-can’t-do-too-many-ruins or can-we-do-more-ruins-in-Asia-than-museums-in-Europe or I-really-like-Tomb-Raider or really, it’s Angkor Wat, you *have* to go) as we were starting to feel schedule pinch and wanted to do this and even more Thailand before we headed further around the world, so no extended Cambodia or Vietnam sightseeing for us. After paying our bribe/entry tax to Cambodia (I also have vague recollections of having *another* Swiss Army knife confiscated and might need to refresh my memory with Danika) we found a lovely civilised guest house, a tuk-tuk driver who was a fully qualified Doctor who couldn’t get work due to some historical war baggage and set off to see some ruins, ending our first day with Angkor Wat at sunset. A week.

Oodles of Noodles – 23rd November 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[No Name Internet Cafe, Sukhothai, Thailand]

Aaahhh…we are really getting into the pace of Thailand. It’s taken a while for us to chill out after our mad pace around Europe but life moves slowly here. We have spent the last week exploring Northern Thailand, and are loathe to leave it… but the beach beckons.

We spent the better part of the last week in and around Chiang Mai, where you heard from us last. Chiang Mai is just great and we only left because there wasn’t really anything left for us to do there. On Sunday, we went for a three day, two night trek in the hills a few hours north of town. We experienced gorgeous untouched scenery, visited native hill tribes, went bamboo rafting and elephant riding! Our elephant was particularly cantankerous, though, and dragged us through thorny trees wherever possible. We also felt extremely OLD as everyone was at least a couple years younger than Danika. However, despite our current state of fitness (much reduced from our marathon peak) we were pleasantly surprised at how easy the trekking itself was, and we were actually wishing the trip had been a bit harder!

Back in Chiang Mai, we took nice hot showers and had massages for our sore muscles (we’re SO unfit!). Our cooking course was such a success the first time, that we went back for another day and made, most notably, Massaman curry. A very tasty experience. We were also lucky enough to be in Chiang Mai for the annual Loi Krathong festival. This occurs on the 2nd full moon of the Thai calendar year and involves parades with extrememly elaborate floats which are sent down the river. There were also TONS of amateur fireworks (every kid in town had a pocket full of ’em!) and lots of floating balloons and flowers with candles in them. Not to mention tons of food vendors. We managed to catch two evenings of this visually stunning festival.

Finally, we made our way down to Sukhothai, between Chiang Mai and Bangkok. It’s a former capital of Thailand and has all kinds of incredible temple ruins featuring some of the most photgraphed Buddhas in Thailand. Today we spent the day exploring them as well as rice fields and the surrounding areas. Perhaps the most exciting part of it all was our first experience on a motorbike! Gordon drove with Danika behind and he quickly discovered (much to Danika’s chagrin) that it was very similar to riding a mountain bike! Danika practiced on a back country road but G was the real bike man and carted us all over the countryside.

Tomorrow we’re off to Ayuthaya, near Bangkok, to see more temple ruins, and then probably continuing on to Cambodia for a few days. We send happy holiday greetings home to all the Americans – we’ll be sure to eat a lot of SOMETHING in honor of Thanksgiving. In the mean time, warm wishes to you all and please do write and let us know what’s happening with you.

D & G
